
## ★ NEUROLOGY ★

### ★ Trigeminal Neuralgia
- **RF:** Elderly & HTN
- **C/p:** Knife-like or electrical shock or lightning pain — painful to chew food — on face specially mandibular division
- **TTT:** First: Carbamazepine — Second: Amitriptyline

---

### ★ Post Herpetic Neuralgia
- **RF:** Hx of rash → especially herpes zoster
- **C/p:** Pain & Allodynia
- **TTT:** First 72h → Acyclovir; after 72h → Analgesics

---

### ★ Intracranial Bleeding
- **RF:** Elderly & HTN — pts on anticoagulants
- **C/p:** Sudden severe headache — loss of consciousness
- First step direct → CT without contrast → white lesion
- **TTT:** Lower & monitor BP

---

### ★ Guillain-Barré Syndrome
- Infection → Immune reaction
- Most common organism: Campylo. Jejuni → diarrhea
- **C/p:** Ascending paralysis — in legs, ascends to arms
- ★ **Areflexia & normal sensation** ★
- **InV:** CSF → Cytoalbuminous → ↑protein & Normal cell count
- **TTT:** Supportive — if severe → IVIG
- **Complication:** Resp. failure → Monitor → Lung vital capacity

---


### ★ Myasthenia Gravis
- **RF:** Female around 30
- **C/p:** Marked **muscle** weakness at the **end of the day**
- **4Ds:** ①Dropping eyelid (asymmetrical) ②Diplopia ③Dysphagia ④Dysphonia
- **InV:** Serum Anti-AChR — Best: Edrophonium test
- CT chest to rule out Thymoma & TSH for thyrotoxicosis
- **TTT:**
  1. Neostigmine & Physostigmine
  2. Immunosuppressant steroids
  3. Acute attack → Plasmapheresis & Corticosteroids
  4. Severe → Thymectomy / Severe → Resp. Failure → **First: Intubation**

---

### ★ Steroid Induced Myopathy
- **RF:** Prolonged high dose steroid
- **C/p:** Proximal muscle weakness (difficulty climbing stairs)

---

### ★ Subarachnoid Hemorrhage
- **RF:** Trauma & HTN
- Most common cause → Rupture of Berry aneurysm
- **C/p:** Worst headache ever — Sudden — Thunderclap headache
- **Sequelae:** Neck stiffness — Papilloedema
- **InV:**
  - First direct: CT non-contrast
  - If normal → 12h LP → RBCs & Xanthochromia
  - CT angio → IOC for aneurysm and for surgery 
- **TTT:** Medically: control HTN — CCBs for vasospasm; Coiling or stenting endovascular therapy
- **Common description:** ①Occipital pain ②Hit across back of the head ③Max severity first 60 sec to 3 minutes

---


### ★ Multiple Sclerosis
- **RF:** Family Hx & women 20–40
- **C/p:**
  1. Most early: Optic neuritis — Painful eye movement — Temp vision loss — Visual disorder — Diplopia — ↓color vision
  2. Rest → wide variety of neurological symptoms: Numbness — Ataxia — Spasticity — Nystagmus — Gait issues 
- **InV:**
  - 1st → MRI → demyelination
  - 2nd → CSF → oligoclonal bands
  - 3rd → Visual evoked response
- **TTT:** 
- Acute/optic neuritis → High dose IV steroid; 
- Prevent future: B-Interferon — Glatiramer acetate

---

### ★ Hemi Neglect $
- Right parietal lobe — Non-dominant hemisphere
- Responds only to stimuli from Rt side
- Shave only Rt side / Comb Rt side
- Not able to copy pentagon

### ★ Gerstmann $ (Gliobastoma)
- Left temporal/parietal lobes — Dominant hemisphere
- No write — No read
- No calculate
- No left — No right
- CT: Butterfly lesion

---

### ★ Restless Leg $
- Old — Iron deficiency anemia
- Urge to move legs - Improves with Alcohol 
- **InV:** Iron studies
- **TTT:** Levodopa

### ★ Essential Tremors
- Old — Iron deficiency anemia
- Tremors increase while stretching close to the object
- **TTT:** BB & Primidone

---

### ★ Creutzfeldt-Jakob
- Rapidly progressive dementia & mycolonus
- EEG: Triphasic synchronous discharge sharp

### ★ Brain Death
- Corneal-gag loss — Dilated pupils
- Apnea → **Most important test**
- Must be confirmed by two physicians 

---


### ★ Cord Cavitation (Syringomyelia)
- Areflexia
- **C/p:** Anesthesia & paresthesia in upper extremities only
- CAPE distribution: Both arms — hands & chest
- **InV:** MRI — **TTT:** Surgery

---

### ★ Diabetic Neuropathy
- Peripheral polyneuropathy & CN3 affection 
- ★ **Stock & gloves anesthesia** ★
- Cause → Ischemia
- **TTT:** Pain control → Pregabalin

### ★ Diabetic Amyotrophy
- Asymmetrical weakness
- Wasting in lower legs, hips with distal neuropathy

---

## ★ HEADACHE ★

### ★ Migraine
- **Classical triad:** Unilateral + Throbbing + Episodic (4–72h)
- **Association C/p:** ①Photophobia — Nausea — Vomiting
- **TTT:**
  - First acute attack: NSAIDs + Antiemetics
  - If failed → Sumatriptan
  - If failed → Ergotamine
- **Prevention:** ①Pizotifen is **first line** ② BB — CCB — TCAs

---

### ★ Cluster
- **Classical triad:** Unilateral + Retroorbital + Ipsilateral eye $
- More in Men & at night
- **C/p:** Ipsilateral eye redness — Tearing eye pain — Horner $ — Stuffed/runny nose
- **TTT Acute:** High flow O₂ — Subcutaneous Sumatriptan
- **Prevention:** Verapamil — Topiramate

---

### ★ Tension
- Band-like: Forehead — Temple & Occipit (radiation)
- No association, No vomiting, No Nausea
- TTT: ①**first line** : massage and relaxation ② NSAIDs

---
### ★ Parkinsonism
- Triad : Elderly + tremors at rest + rigidity
- **C/p:** bill rolling tremors — Shuffling gait — Rigidity against passive movement — Bradykinesia — Masked face — Monotonous speech - Micrographia
- **TTT:** ①Levodopa ②Benztropine for tremors

---

## ★ SEIZURES ★

### ★ Grand Mal — Tonic-Clonic Seizures
- **Tonic-clonic phase:** ①LOC ②Stiffening ③Jerking ④Tongue biting ⑤Urinary & fecal incontinence
- **Post-ictal phase:** ①Flaccid unresponsive ②Gradual return of awareness (15 min)

---

### ★ Petit Mal — Absence Seizure
- **RF:** Children — Family Hx 
- **Seizure characteristics:**
  1. No motor manifestation
  2. Impaired consciousness (5–10 sec) (day dreaming in school)
  3. Hyperventilation *before*
  4. Amnesia *after*
- **InV:** EEG → Three per second spike
- **TTT:** Ethosuximide — Valproic acid

---

### ★ Infantile Spasm
- Bilateral symmetrical contraction
- **Age:** 5–6 months
- **C/p:** Spasms in neck muscles — extremities & trunk
- **EEG:** Hypsarrhythmia
- **TTT:** High dose steroids

---

### ★ Febrile Convulsions
- Same as generalized Grand mal in child
- **Age:** 6 months – 6 years — Hx of infection
- **C/p:** Fever
- **TTT:** Control fever: Paracetamol — Ice packs; 
- Last line → Diazepam

---
### ★ Juvenile Myoclonic Epilepsy
- Mid-to-late childhood
- **C/p:** Clonic seizures without LOC, often during morning or at lack of sleep or flickering lights
- **EEG:** Generalized polyspike
- **TTT:** Valproic acid is the most effective anti-epileptic drug 

---
### ★ Temporal Lobe Epilepsy
- Focal, partial seizure
- **C/p:** ①Preceding aura ②Focal impaired awareness (30–60 sec) ③Fixed stare ④Lip smacking → followed by confusion
- **TTT:** First → Carbamazepine; Second → Sodium Valproate

---
## ★ Assessing fitness for driving ★
### ★ Seizures Driving Rules: 6 – 12 – 3
- First attack → **6 months**
- More than 1 or recurrent → **12 months**
- Seizure in controlled patient over a year → **3 months**

---
### ★ Hypoglycemia
- Mild hypoglycemia → give advices
- Severe hypoglycemia LOC → **6 weeks**

### ★ Dementia
- Conditional license for **1 year** → drive around home & closed areas

### ★ Other Medical Conditions
1. Syncope → 1st: 6 months — recurrent → 12 months
2. MI or PCI → 2 weeks
3. CABG or aneurysm repair → 4 weeks
4. Subarachnoid Hge → 3 months
5. TIA & Stroke → 2 weeks & 4 weeks respectively

- **Cause:** Otolith → debris repositioning
- **C/p:** Vertigo related to position → **Keyword**
- **InV:** Dix-Hallpike maneuver
- **TTT:** Epley's Maneuver

## ★ STROKE ★

### Risk Factors
1. **Atherosclerosis:** Most common — by thrombus & distal emboli
2. **Embolic:** Usually from AF → Mostly posterior cerebral artery
3. **HTN:** Hemorrhagic

---

★ Clinical features according to location & severity ★
### ★ TIA
- Sudden focal neurological deficit **< 24 hours**
- 20% of the patients have recurrent stroke within 90 days
- **C/p:** Unilateral weakness — speech disturbance — loss of vision
- Duration: Minutes to hours then **complete recovery**

---

### ★ MCA — Affect UL more than LL
*(May lead to brain swelling & herniation)*
- **C** → ①Contralateral Hemiparesis & facial weakness
- **H** → ②Homonymous hemianopia & eye deviation to affected side (gaze to affected lesion)
- **A** → ③Aphasia
- **N** → ④Neglect syndrome & hemi sensory loss

---

### ★ ACA — Affect LL more than UL / Affects frontal lobe
1. Contralateral hemiparesis & sensory loss in legs
2. Personality changes — Apathy & Abulia & expressive aphasia
3. Incontinence & Apraxia

---
### ★ ICA
1. ★ Sudden headache
2. ★ **Ipsilateral blindness**
3. ★ Convulsions
4. Contralateral hemiparesis & hemianesthesia 
5. Aphasia 

---
### ★ Vertebrobasilar Artery — Brain stem + cerebellum + visual cortex
1. Coma
2. Drop attacks
3. Ataxia — Tremors — Vertigo — Nausea & vomiting
4. Cranial nerves 4Ds: ①Diplopia ②Dysphagia ③Dysarthria ④7th nerve
5. Crossed weakness & sensory loss: ipsilateral face, contralateral body

---

### ★ PICA / Wallenberg / Lateral Medullary $
- **Ipsilateral face:** ①Horner syndrome ②Nystagmus ③Ataxia ④4Ds ⑤Absent corneal reflex ⑥Facial numbness
- **Contralateral body:** ①Hemiplegic ②Spinothalamic sensory loss

---

### ★ Lesion in Internal Capsule
- uncrossed hemiplegia : Contralateral hemiplegia & sensory loss + Contralateral weakness of the lower half of the face
- Lacunar infarction: Hemiplegia + burning sensation
### **★ Cerebellum**
Tremor — Ataxia & Nystagmus

### **Exam Rules** :

- **Posterior/Brainstem (Crossed Signs) ⚖️:** * **Rule:** Ipsilateral (same side) face + Contralateral (opposite) body = Brainstem.
    - **The "Coma" Split:** You are right. Basilar artery involvement often leads to coma or "Locked-in syndrome". PICA (Wallenberg) patients are usually awake but have vertigo and dysphagia (swallowing issues).
     
- **Internal Capsule (Same Side) 🧠:** * **Rule:** Face and body are both affected on the **same side** (contralateral to the lesion).
    
    - **Exam Tip:** Look for "pure motor" or "pure sensory" descriptions without any "higher cortical" signs like aphasia.
    
- **ACA (The Leg) 🦵:** * **Rule:** Leg weakness is much worse than arm weakness.
    
    - **Bonus:** Check for urinary incontinence or personality changes to confirm.
    
- **MCA (The Arm/Face) 💪:** * **Rule:** Arm and face weakness are much worse than leg weakness.
    
    - **Vision vs. Eye:** In MCA, it's a **field defect** (Homonymous hemianopia), meaning they can't see out of one side of their world.
    
- **ICA (The Eye vs. Body) 👁️:** * **Rule:** **Ipsilateral** blindness (the actual eye goes dark) + **Contralateral** body weakness.
    
    - **Exam Differentiator:** If the question says "blindness in the left eye" and "weakness in the right arm," it's ICA. If it says "cannot see the right half of the world," it's MCA.

### ★ Affection of Visual Pathway
1. Unilateral blindness → Optic nerve
2. Bitemporal hemianopia → Optic chiasma
3. Temporal loss & nasal loss → Homonymous hemianopia → Optic tract lesion — occipital lobe/optic radiation
4. Upper quadratic → Homonymous quadrantanopia → Temporal lobe
5. Lower quadratic → Homonymous quadrantanopia → Parietal lobe

---
### ★ Subacute Combined Degeneration
- **Cause:** Vit B12 deficiency
- Degeneration of the posterior column
- **C/p:** ①Loss of position & vibration — loss of balance ②Unsteady gait ③Tingling & numbness
- **InV:** +ve Romberg sign → **Keyword**

---

### ★ MND (Motor Neuron Disease) 
- Damage motor neurons → Muscles are damaged
- ALS → Most common type
- **C/p:** Both UMNL & LMNL: ①Hyperreflexia & Hypertonia ②Muscle weakness & Fasciculation
- **InV:** No sensory loss — EMG — Nerve conduction studies
- **TTT:** Riluzole

---
### ★ Duchenne Dystrophy
- X-linked recessive in dystrophin gene
- → Gower sign → climb himself
- **InV:** ↑CK — (Best) Biopsy + initial

---
### ★ Myotonia Dystrophy
- Pts hold doctor's hand & don't let go
- Delayed relaxation & prolonged contraction of skeletal muscle

---

### ★ Friedreich Ataxia — Children ataxia
- **Age:** 10–15 → **Keyword**
- **C/p:** Ataxia — gait instability — difficulty walking
  - With ①Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy ②Kyphoscoliosis ③Areflexia & slurred speech

---

### ★ Charcot Marie Tooth
- Degeneration of peripheral nerves
- **C/p:** Wasting in leg & arms — foot drop — High foot arches & clawed toes — loss of limb sensation
- **InV:** Next → NCV. Best → Biopsy

---

### ★ Neurogenic Shock
- Hypotension + Bradycardia
- **Causes:** Trauma to nervous system or epidural anesthesia
- **MgMt:** First fluids → 2nd vasopressors → 3rd Atropine

---

### ★ Prolactinoma
- Amenorrhea + Galactorrhea
- Male → Gynecomastia + decreased libido + ED
- **Inv:** Prolactin → above 1000; 2nd Step → MRI imaging
- **TTT:** Medical: Bromocriptine or Cabergoline
- Surgery if medical failed → Transsphenoidal

---
### ★ Pituitary Apoplexy
- **(Headache + Diplopia → CT)**
- **RF:** Pregnancy
- **C/p:** Headache — Diplopia — Nausea & vomiting
- **InV:** First → CT. Best → MRI
- **TTT:** Urgent steroid injection & Transsphenoidal decompression

---

### ★ Bell's Palsy
- Acute unilateral LMNL of facial nerve
- **C/p:** Ipsilateral loss of wrinkles — closure of the eye — deviation of mouth to normal side
- **InV:** MRI if tumor suspected → parotid/Malignant (rare)
- **TTT:** Protect eye w/ artificial tears — Steroids
- ★ N.B. LMNL → whole side of the face (ipsilateral)
- ★ N.B. UMNL → only lower ½ of the face (contralateral)

## ★ Cranial Nerves ★

| Nerve       | Features                                                                     |
| ----------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Optic       | Loss of vision & light reflex                                                |
| Oculomotor  | Motor → Ptosis & Squint; Occulo → Mydriasis & loss of light reflex; Diplopia |
| Trochlear   | Vertical diplopia on looking **downward**                                    |
| Abducent    | Horizontal diplopia on looking left & right; same side of nerve palsy        |
| Hypoglossal | Deviation of tongue to the **same side** of the lesion                       |

---
### ★ Radial Nerve Lesion

**At axilla:**
1. Wrist drop
2. Weak elbow extension
3. Weak finger extension
4. Lost triceps reflex
- Sensory → over dorsum of 1st finger-snuffbox *(Post interosseous injury)*

**At spiral groove:**
1. Wrist drop
2. Weak finger extension
- Sensory → over dorsum of 1st finger-snuffbox

**At forearm** *(Radial fracture):*
1. Weak finger extension
2. **No** wrist drop
- Sensation → Normal

---

### ★ Ulnar Nerve Lesion

**At elbow:**
1. Partial claw hand
2. Loss of abduction & adduction
3. Loss of adduction of thumb
4. Flattening of hypothenar
- Sensation → Medial ⅓ of palm — medial 1½ fingers

**At wrist:**
1. Complete claw hand
2. Loss of abduction & adduction
3. Loss of adduction of thumb
4. Flattening of hypothenar
- Sensation: Medial ⅓ of palm — Medial 1½ fingers
- **Test:** place paper between finger & grasp

---
### ★ Median Nerve
- Supracondylar fracture / carpal tunnel
1. Wasting of thenar eminence
2. Ape's hand
3. Weak flexion of wrist
4. No flexion or abduction or opposition of thumb
- **Sensory:** Loss of sensation 3½ lateral & lateral ⅔ of the palm
