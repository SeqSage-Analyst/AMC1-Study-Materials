# Nephrology (Image 1)

* **Nephritic $:** Glomerulonephritis
    1)  **post streptococcal:** RF: 1) children 2) Hx of infection weeks ago
        * organism: Group A B hemolytic streptococci
        * c/p: 1) oliguria 2) Hematuria ~> Tea urine 3) HTN 4) Edema - periorbital swelling
        * Inv: First: Urine analysis Next: ASOT
            Best: renal biopsy
        * TTT: 1) restrict salt - ↑↑ carbs - ↓↓ protien
            2) control BP & Fluid restriction
    2)  **IgA Nephropathy:** RF: Hx of infection 1-2 days
        * c/p: Hematuria - HTN
        * INV: urin analysis & Renal biopsy
    3)  **Thin basement membrane:** Most common cause benign hematuria
        * c/p: Hematuria - NO HTN - NO edema
        * TTT: only reassurance

* **Hemolytic uremic $:** RF: Hx of bloody diarrhea
    * Causitive organism: E.Coli O157-H7
    * c/p: pallor - oliguria - HTN - Edema
    * Inv: renal insufficiency

* **Thrombotic thrombocytopenic $:** RF: Hx of bloody diarrhea
    * c/p: CNS symptoms ± fever
    * labs: ↑↑ KF, shistocytes & low platelets
    * TTT: Plasmapheresis

# Nephrology Continued (Image 2)

* **Henoch Schoenlein purpura:** IGA vasculitis
    * RF: Hx of infection
    * c/p: Triad: pain - rash - hematuria
    * Complication: Peutz-Jeghers $ ~> intussusception

* **Protien uria:**
    1)  **Nephrotic $:** Most commonly: Minimal change
        * c/p: periorbital swelling - Marked edema
        * Inv: 3 ~> 1) Hypoalbuinemia 2) >3.5g protien uria 3) Hyperlipidemia
        * *Normal Renal function*
        * Complications:
            1) Thrombophillia ~> Renal vein thrombosis
               *Nephrotic + Abd pain + HTN ~> duplex & anticoagulant*
            2) Most common in P ~> spontaneous bacte peritonitis
        * MgMt: 1) ↑↑ protien 2) ↓↓ salt & Fluid 3) lasix - furosamide

* **Vesico-ureteral reflux:**
    * c/p: recurrent UTI since childhood -> **Keyword**
    * Complication: recurrent pyelonephritis
    * Inv: VCUG -> Inv of choice
        renal scar -> DMSA
    * TTT: prophylactic antibiotics continously

* **Hepato renal $:** Kidney failure secondary to live failure
    * TTT: octreotide, albumin & midodrine

    * c/p: Hematuria & Hemaptysis
    * Inv: Best: Renal biopsy
        Screening: antibasement membrane antibody

    * c/p: Blue/purple discoloration of finger & toes

    * labs: ↑↑ renin
    * Inv: Next duplex
        Best angiography
    * **TTT:**
        * unilateral: ACEI
        * Bilateral: angioplasty & stent

# Electrolytes Disturbance (Image 4)

**1) Sodium:**
Normal: 135 - 145
* **Hypernatremia:** Most common cause ~> dehydration
    * c/p: Confusion - convulsions & coma
    * TTT: gradual correction 48-72h
* **Hyponatremia:** Most common cause ~> SIADH
    * c/p: Confusion - seizures & coma
    * *** Serum osmolarity low
    * TTT: Hyponatremia only -> Normal saline
        Hyponatremia + symptom -> Hypertonic saline (3%)
    * *** Hyperglycemia -> Pseudo hyponatremia

**2) Potassium:**
Normal: 3.5 - 5
* **Hyperkalemia:** Most common cause: Impaired renal excretion
    * c/p: Heart -> Fatal arrhythmias & arrest
        Intestine -> colic, areflexia & Flaccid paralysis
    * Inv: ECG -> **Tall peaked T wave**
    * Complication -> V.fib & cardiac arrest
    * **TTT & Types:**
        1)  **Severe:** K > 7.0 - ECG disturbance
            Calcium gluconate - Salb(neb) - Insulin/glucose
            * Bicarb: Metabolic acidosis - Resonium
        2)  **Moderate:** K 6-7 - Normal ECG
            Salb(neb) - Insulin/glucose - Resonium (kayxalate)
        3)  **Mild:** K 5.5-6 - Asymptomatic
            Stop any K supplements - stop offending drugs

# Electrolytes Continued & SIADH (Image 5)

* **Hypokalemia:** causes: GI loss - Renal loss
    * c/p: Muscle weakness - cramps - fatigue
    * Inv: ECG: Flat T wave - U waves
    * TTT: potassium repletion

**3) Calcium:**
Normal: 2.2 - 2.5
* **Hypercalcemia:** Most common cause: hyperparathyroidism
    * Inv: First: urine calcium & serum parathormone level
        ECG: Short QT interval
    * TTT: emergency: I V Fluid & Furosamide
        calcitonin & bisphosphonate
* **Hypocalcemia:** Most common: hypoparathyroidism (post op)
    * c/p: Tetany & perioral numbness
    * Inv: 1) Chvostek's sign 2) Trousseau's sign
    * labs: Ionized Ca+2, Mg+2, PTH & Albumin
    * TTT: Mild: Ca+2 1.9 - 2.2 -> oral calcium supplements
        Severe: Ca+2 < 1.9 -> I V calcium

* **SIADH:** ↑↑ ADH (less water out) (More water in)
    1)  Euvolemic hyponatremia < 135
    2)  Urine osmolality: High
    3)  Na in urine: High
    4)  Serum osmolality: low
    * c/p -> TTT: similar to hyponatremia
    * 1st step: restriction of Fluid
    * chronic SIADH -> demeclocyclin

# ABG Interpretation (Image 6)

* **ABG:**
    1 - look pH & PCO2 with or against
        * with: Metabolic
        * against: Respiratory
    2 - look pH high or low
        * High: Alkalosis
        * Low: acidosis
    3 - look HCO3 & PCO2 with or against then pH
        * with & Normal pH: Compensated
        * against: Uncompensated
        * with & abnormal pH: partially compensated

* **Vomiting** -> Metabolic alkalosis
* **Diarrhea** -> Metabolic acidosis
* **Hyperventilation** -> respiratory alkalosis
