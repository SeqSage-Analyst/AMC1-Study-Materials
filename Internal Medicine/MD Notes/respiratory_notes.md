## Bronchial Asthma
> Child + wheezes + nocturnal cough

### Clinical Picture
- Dry cough + SOB + nocturnal symptoms
- **Exam:** Wheezes with prolonged expiration

### Investigations
- **< 7 years** → improvement after salbutamol
- **≥ 7 years** → spirometry → ↓ FEV1

### Management

#### Acute Attack
- Low O₂ saturation → give O₂
1. Inhaled salbutamol × 3 doses every 20 minutes
2. Oral cortisone (if no response)

#### Long-term
1. SABA
2. Inhaled cortisone
3. LABA

## Pulmonary Embolism
> Risk factors: pregnancy, bedridden, post-operative, long flights

### Clinical Picture
- Sudden dyspnea + chest pain

### Investigations
- **Wells score → Likely** → CTPA
- **Wells score → Unlikely** → D-dimer → if +ve → CTPA
- **V/Q scan** if renal impairment or pregnancy

### Management

| Phase | Duration | Treatment |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Acute | 5–14 days | LMWH (or UFH if renal impairment) |
| Maintenance | 3–6 months | Warfarin + heparin until INR = 2.0 → then warfarin alone |

> **If anticoagulation contraindicated** → Emergency pulmonary embolectomy

## Pneumonia
> Fever + chest pain + absent breath sounds

### Clinical Picture
- SOB, fever, cough with sputum

### Exam
1. Dullness
2. Absent breath sounds
3. Increased TVF (↑↑)

### Investigations
- Chest X-ray + blood culture

### Management (CURB-65)
- **Not severe** → oral amoxicillin or IV penicillin
- **Severe** → IV ceftriaxone + IV flucloxacillin
- **MRSA** → add vancomycin
- **Atypical** → doxycycline or erythromycin

## Mycoplasma Pneumonia

### Clinical Picture
- Fever, dry cough, erythema multiforme

### Investigations
- **CXR:** bilateral interstitial infiltrates
- **Blood:** hemolytic anemia

### Treatment
- Doxycycline or azithromycin

---

## Legionella Pneumonia
> Associated with air conditioning

### Clinical Picture
- Fever + respiratory symptoms + **hyponatremia** + confusion

### Investigations
1. CXR → bilateral interstitial infiltrates
2. Increased LFTs

## Pneumothorax
> Air in pleural space

### Clinical Picture
- Sudden SOB + chest pain
- **Exam:** absent breath sounds + hyperresonance

### Management by Type

| Type | Management |
|------|------------|
| Tension | Needle decompression |
| Traumatic + symptomatic | Chest tube |
| Traumatic + asymptomatic | O₂ + follow-up X-ray |
| Primary + symptoms | Aspiration (regardless of size) |
| Primary + no symptoms | O₂ + follow-up |
| Secondary + symptoms | Chest tube (regardless of size) |
| Secondary + > 50% | Chest tube |
| Secondary + < 50% | Aspiration → if failed → chest drain |
| Secondary + < 25% + no symptoms | Conservative |

## Pleural Effusion
> Fluid accumulation

### Clinical Picture
- SOB, dry cough, reduced chest expansion

### Investigations — Thoracentesis (Light's Criteria)

| Ratio | < Threshold | ≥ Threshold |
|-------|------------|-------------|
| Pleural fluid protein / serum protein | < 0.5 → **Transudate** | ≥ 0.5 → **Exudate** |
| Pleural fluid LDH / serum LDH | < 0.6 → **Transudate** | ≥ 0.6 → **Exudate** |

### Treatment
- Chest tube if needed

## COPD
> Risk factors: smoking, old age, recurrent infections

### Clinical Picture
- Chronic cough, SOB, chest tightness
- **Exam:** hyperresonance, expiratory wheezes, crepitations

### Investigations
- **First:** spirometry
- **X-ray:** hyperinflation, narrow cardiac shadow

### Management
1. Smoking cessation
2. O₂ therapy
3. Bronchodilators

### Complications
- **Infection:** yellow sputum + fever → O₂ + antibiotics
- **Sudden chest pain** → suspect pneumothorax → CXR + chest tube
- **Severe dyspnea in ER** → O₂ if hypoxic → ABG (low-flow O₂)

## Bronchiectasis
> Permanent bronchial dilation

### Risk Factors
- Recurrent infections, TB, COPD

### Clinical Picture
- Chronic productive cough + excessive mucus
- **Exam:** wheezing + coarse crackles

### Investigations
- CT → honeycomb, tram-track, signet ring signs

### Management
- Amoxicillin-clavulanate or doxycycline

### Key Associations
- Acute bronchitis → acute hemoptysis
- Chronic bronchitis → bronchiectasis
- Immotile cilia syndrome → bronchiectasis + male infertility
- Immotile cilia + dextrocardia → **Kartagener syndrome**

## Tuberculosis (TB)
> Risk factors: immigrants, healthcare workers

### Clinical Picture
- Night sweats, fever, weight loss, loss of appetite

### Investigations
- **Asymptomatic:** Mantoux / Quantiferon → if positive → CXR
- **Symptomatic:** CXR → confirm with sputum culture (3 samples)

### Management

| Test Result | X-ray Result | Action |
|-------------|--------------|--------|
| +ve | +ve | Isolation + **RIPE** |
| −ve | −ve | BCG vaccine |
| +ve | −ve | Isoniazid (6–9 months) |
| Symptomatic | — | Isolation + **RIPE** |

## Bronchogenic Carcinoma
> Elderly smoker

### Clinical Picture
- Cough, dyspnea, hemoptysis, weight loss

### Types
- Squamous cell carcinoma (SCC)
- Adenocarcinoma
- Small cell (associated with paraneoplastic syndromes)

---

## Pancoast Tumor
> Apical lung cancer

### Clinical Picture
- Chest pain

### Complications (by compression)
- **Brachial plexus** → weakness, atrophy
- **Sympathetic chain** → Horner's syndrome
- **Veins** → neck vein congestion

### Investigations
- **First:** CT
- **Best:** bronchoscopy + biopsy

---

## Lung Fibrosis (Idiopathic)

### Clinical Picture
- Chest symptoms
- **Exam:** clubbing, cyanosis, fine crackles, basal crepitations

### Investigations
- **X-ray:** bilateral interstitial pattern
- **CT:** investigation of choice
- **ABG:** hypoxia
- **Spirometry:** restrictive pattern (↓ all values)

### Treatment
- Corticosteroids + immunosuppressants
