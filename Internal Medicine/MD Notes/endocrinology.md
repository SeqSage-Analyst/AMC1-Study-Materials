# Endocrinology

## Thyrotoxicosis
**C/P:** 
1. AF 
2. tremors 
3. heat intolerance

**InV:** 
* TSH: decreased,  if increased → pituitary adenoma
* T3 & T4: increased
* Antibodies → TSAb → Grave's
* Radioactive:
    * → increased → Homogenous → Grave's
    * → increased → Heterogenous → Plummer
    * → decreased → painful → De Quervain's

**TTT:**
* Medication → Carbimazole - propylthiouracil
* Radiotherapy → in recurrence after surgery
* Surgery → Total thyroidectomy
* Thyroiditis → Aspirin only

## Hypothyroidism
**C/P:**
1. Fatigue
2. Cold intolerance
3. Expressionless face
4. loss outer 2/3 eyebrows with puffy eyelid
5. Sinus Bradycardia
6. Constipation

**InV:**
* T3 & T4: decreased
* TSH:
    * Primary Hashimoto's → increased
    * Secondary post op → decreased
    * Iodine uptake → decreased
    * Chemistry → cholesterol → increased

***Note:** Low T3 & T4 - Normal TSH → pituitary adenoma*

**MgMt: Thyroxine**
* → 50-100 ug/day
* → CVD → 25 then increase 25
* Monitor therapy → TSH then T4 4 weeks after TTT

# Differential Diagnosis of Thyroid Disorders 

| Condition                                   | TSH level  | T3 & T4 levels    | RAIU Scan Pattern                    | Key Clinical Pearls & Investigations                                                                      |
| :------------------------------------------ | :--------- | :---------------- | :----------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Grave's Disease**                         | ↓ Low      | ↑ High            | **Diffusely increased** (Homogenous) | Positive TSAb (TRAb). Often accompanied by exophthalmos.                                                  |
| **Plummer's Disease** *(Toxic MNG)*         | ↓ Low      | ↑ High            | **Patchy / Heterogenous** increased  | Palpable nodules on exam.                                                                                 |
| **Subacute Thyroiditis** *(De Quervain's)*  | ↓ Low      | ↑ High            | **Decreased / Low**                  | **Painful / tender** thyroid. Triggered by viral infection. Treat with Aspirin/NSAIDs.                    |
| **Pituitary Adenoma** *(TSH-secreting)*     | **↑ High** | ↑ High            | Diffusely increased                  | Secondary hyperthyroidism. **Dx:** Requires MRI Brain to confirm.                                         |
| **Exogenous Thyrotoxicosis** *(Factitious)* | ↓ Low      | ↑ High            | **Decreased / Low**                  | Surreptitious levothyroxine use. Exam shows an **atrophic gland**. Labs: Low thyroglobulin.               |
| **Sick Euthyroid Syndrome** *(SES)*         | Normal / ↓ | **↓ Low T3 only** | Not indicated                        | Seen in **acute severe illness** (e.g., ICU patients) due to decreased peripheral conversion of T4 to T3. |
| **Hashimoto's Thyroiditis**                 | ↑ High     | ↓ Low             | Variable (often low)                 | Hypothyroidism. Gland appears **heterogenous** on ultrasound. Positive Anti-TPO antibodies.               |

## Diabetes
**Diagnosis of diabetes symptoms (+):**
1. FBG ≥ 7.0
2. HbA1C ≥ 6.5
3. RBG ≥ 11.1

**Diagnosis of diabetes in Asymptomatic:**
1. HbA1C ≥ 6.5 on two separate occasions
2. FBG ≥ 7.0 or RBG ≥ 11.1 - 2 times (repeat the same test)
3. OGTT → fasting ≥ 7.0, two-hour post ≥ 11.1

## DKA (Diabetic Ketoacidosis):
**Causes:** infection - missed insulin dose
**C/P:** 
1. Dehydration 
2. dry skin 
3. Abd pain 
4. vomiting 
5. Kussmaul breathing → compensate metabolic acidosis

**InV:**
* First step → ABG → Metabolic acidosis
* Blood → Acetone & hyperglycemia
* Urine → glucose & Ketons

**TTT:**
1. IV Fluid 
2. insulin 
3. K after passing urine

## Hypoglycemia:
**C/P:** irritability - tremors - Sweating - Tachy & HTN
**TTT:**
1. oral glucose
* if unconscious → IV glucose (25ml 50%)
* in resistant or no IV line access → IM glucagon

## Acromegaly:
**↑ growth hormone → pituitary adenoma**
**C/P:** big everything - uncontrolled HTN & DM
**InV:** Insulin growth hormone
* confirmatory: Glucose inhibit GH
* MRI → pituitary lesion
**TTT:** Somatostatin, cabergolin or bromocriptine
**Complication:** CHF → Most common cause of death, Colon Cancer

## Hypoparathyroidism:
**cause**: post thyroidectomy → ↓ Ca+2
**C/P:** hypocalcemia → perioral numbness, carpopedal spasm
**InV:** 1. Chvostek & Trousseau test
2. ECG → prolonged QT interval
**TTT:** IV Calcium gluconate, long term → vit D & Ca+2
## Hyperparathyroidism:
**↑ PTH → cause → adenoma → ↑ Ca+2**
**C/P:** Groans - bones - Moans - Stones
**InV:** Most imp → Radioactive studies (Parathyroid Scan)
* labs: ↑ Ca+2, ↑ PTH, ↑ urine Ca+2, ↓ PO4
**TTT:** Surgery
* *Note: Hypercalcemia → First inv → PTH then Ca*
* *Note: Hyperparathyroidism → First inv → parathyroid scan*
## Osteomalacia: 
**Vit D deficiency in adults**
* ↓ Vit D → ↓ Ca+2, ↓ PO4
* Bone ache & tenderness
**InV:** Bilateral Symmetric pseudo fracture
**TTT:** Vit D supplements

## Addison disease:
**Adrenal insufficiency - ↓ Na - ↑ K**
**Cause:** Autoimmune & TB
**C/P:** pigmentation → recent scars, Buccal mucosa & tongue
* Anorexia - Vomiting & abd pain
* Hyponatremia & Hyperkalemia
**InV:** Cosyntropin
**TTT:** Steroids

**Addison Crises:** Sudden withdrawal of cortisone
* **C/P:** dehydration - Shock - Abd pain & vomiting
* Hypoglycemia - Hyponatremia - Hypotension - ↑ K
* **TTT:** 1st step → IV hydrocortisone & I.V. fluids

## Conn's:
**↑ Aldosterone**
**C/P:** HTN - ↑ Na - Metabolic Alkalosis - ↓ K
**InV:** Hormonal evaluation then CT
**TTT:** Spironolactone

## Accidental discovery of Adrenal mass:
* First → Hormonal evaluation
* ≤ 4cm → Follow up
* \> 4cm → CT biopsy
* \> 4cm & Normal hormone level → No TTT
## Pheochromocytoma:
**C/P:** Sudden attack of palpitations, Headache, tremors
* Anxiety - panic attack & episodic elevation of BP

**InV:** * First → catecholamines 
* Then → metanephrines & VMA
* Imaging → CT or MRI. Inv of choice → MIBG scan

**TTT:** Phenoxybenzamine to control blood pressure

**D.D:** * Panic attack → psychiatric symptoms → sense of dying
* Pheochromocytoma → High BP & headache
