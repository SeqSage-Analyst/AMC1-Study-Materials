## 1. ECG

### ECG Basics
* P → Atrial contraction
* P-R → Time from Atrial contraction to Ventricular contraction
* QRS → Ventricular Contraction
* T → Ventricular relaxation → Ischemia or MI

### Exam Based Strategy
1. Rate? RR interval? 3-5 large squares
2. Rhythm? Regular or Irregular 
3. P wave - present?
4. QRS - width? 2.5 small squares
   - If all good:
     -  P-R interval
     -  ST segment & T wave

---

### SVT (Supraventricular Tachycardia)
1. Regular Tachy
2. Regular
3. Present P wave
4. Narrow QRS

**Unstable** → Cardioversion 
**Stable** → 1- vagal stimulation  2- IV adenosine (Avoid in Asthma & COPD)

---

### Ventricular Tachycardia
1. Tachy
2. Regular
3. Wide QRS
4. Present P wave

 **Unstable** → Cardioversion 
 Stable → Amiodarone 
 **Pulseless** → Defibrillator

## 3. Heart Block
* Bradycardia - regular - present P & Normal QRS
### PR Interval?
1. **First degree:** equally prolonged PR ≥ 5 small squares. All P waves followed by QRS
2. **Second degree:**
   * Mobitz 1: progressively lengthening PR with dropped beat.
   * Mobitz 2: dropped beat without progressive length of PR. → **TTT even if asymptomatic pacemaker**
1. **Third degree:** Complete dissociation between P & QRS. neck sign → Cannon waves.
   * TTT: unstable → Atropine + pacemaker
   * stable → pacemaker

### Valvular Heart Diseases

#### Mitral Stenosis
* Risk: pregnant & immigrant → rheumatic fever ( most common cause )
- Symptoms: Hemoptysis, Hoarseness (Ortner syndrome from LA enlargement)
- Murmur: Rumbling mid-diastolic murmur + opening snap (Loud S1)
- Complications: AFib → Thrombus → Stroke, Acute mesenteric ischemia, Acute limb ischemiaa
#### Mitral Regurgitation
* Cause: MVP (most common), Post-MI (papillary muscle rupture), Endocarditis
- Murmur: Pansystolic radiating to axilla (+ S3 gallop in severe cases)
- Most imp drug → ACEI
#### Aortic Stenosis
* Cause: Age-related calcification (>65yo), Bicuspid valve (<65yo)
- Symptoms → ↓ COP: SAD (Syncope - Angina - Dyspnea)
- Murmur: Crescendo-decrescendo systolic radiating to the **carotids** (+ Ejection click)
#### Aortic Regurgitation
* Cause: Aortic root dilation (**Marfan**, Syphilis), Bicuspid valve
- Murmur: Early diastolic radiating to the left sternal border radiating to the  **apex**
- Provocation: Increased by leaning forward

### 5. Congenital Heart Disease

### VSD
* Asymptomatic at birth - **(Sudden)** cyanosis weeks later
* Murmur: Pansystolic left 2nd ICS radiating all over myocardium
* Most resolve spontaneously
### ASD
* Murmur: systolic left 2nd ICS & Fixed splitting S2
* Association - > RBBB 
### PDA
* Most common cause: rubella
* Murmur: Continuous Machinery below clavicle
* **Late cyanosis in lower extremities**
### Coarctation of Aorta
* Narrowing in descending aorta - good upper body - bad lower body
* Cpx: well developed upper body - poorly developed lower body
* High BP upper - low BP lower
* **Femoral pulse aren't felt**
* Murmur: systolic radiating to the back
### TOF (Tetralogy of Fallot)
* Most Common Cause of Cyanosis in childhood
* **PROVE**
  * Pulmonary infundibular stenosis
  * Right ventricular hypertrophy
  * Overriding aorta
  * VSD
* CP (Tet spell): bluish skin during crying or feeding
* Cyanosis at childhood - Not at birth
* Complications: hyper cyanotic spell, marked dyspnea & cyanosis
* Mgmt: 1st → knee to chest position
        2nd → O2 - Morphine - sodium bicarbonate
### Transposition of Great Arteries
* Cyanosis **since birth**
* Mgmt: Prostaglandins to maintain ductus arteriosus
### Hypoplastic Left Heart Syndrome
* HF symptoms after few days from birth

### Pericarditis
* Most common cause = **Coxsackievirus**
* Ch.Ch: Stitching Chest pain ↑ respiration & Movement
* **Decreases by leaning forward & sitting**
* ECG: Diffuse ST segment elevation - ST depression AVR, V1
* TTT: NSAIDs - colchicine (3 months) - (6 months) if chronic
* Renal failure → Immediate dialysis

### Constrictive
* Most common cause = TB
* C/P: Systemic congestion - Congested neck veins. Kussmaul sign and Breathing
* Most imp investigation : CT → Calcifications
* TTT: early surgery

### Rheumatic Fever
* Most common cause: Group A strep 
* Risk Factors: Aboriginal & prior pharyngeal infection
* **Jones criteria:** 2 Major - 1 Major + 2 Minor
  * Major: Joints - Carditis - Nodules - Erythema marginatum - Chorea
  * Minor: Fever - Hx of RF - Prolonged PR - ↑ ESR & Arthralgia

* **Inv & labs**
  * 1st → sore throat swab
  * 2nd → Antistreptolysin - ECG - PR

* **TTT:**
  * ① For infection → penicillin 10 days - if allergic → Erythromycin
  * ② prevention: Benzathine penicillin every month

### Endocarditis

#### Causative Organisms
1. Strep. viridians → Most common & after dental procedure
2. Canula - IV drug addict → Staph. aureus
3. GI & Uro procedure → E. faecalis
4. Colon Cancer → Strep. Bovis

#### Clinical Features : FROM JANE
* Very wide & general & clinical suspicion Dx
1. Fever + Cardiac change *Appearance of new murmur* → Keyword
2. One of these: Roth spots in eye, petechial rash, Painful Osler's nodules, Janeway painless macules,  splinter hemorrhage under nails

#### Investigations
* First line → ECHO *Not for screening for pts with fever & +ve blood*
* Best & Confirmatory → TOE *look for vegetation*
* Second → Blood Culture *Yes if suspicion & screening*
#### Management
* Antibiotics course: Amoxicillin + Gentamicin + Ceftriaxone
#### Prophylaxis (2 Conditions)
1. Significant Cardiac defect (cyanotic, Hx of IE & prosthetic valve)
2. dental or invasive respiratory operations or ongoing GI or GU infection 

| **Dental:** Amoxicillin |
| **GI or GU:** Ampicillin |

### HOCM (Hypertrophic Obstructive Cardiomyopathy)
* Left ventricular hypertrophy
* Autosomal dominant
* Risk Factors: Athletes &  Family Hx
* C/p: dyspnea - Chest pain - Syncope - Cardiac arrhythmias ( Vtach and Vfib)
* All increase with exercise, dehydration, diuretics
* Exam: 1- Valsalva maneuver → worsens condition 2- systolic murmur over LT sternal border without radiation ( AS → Systolic murmur over aortic area radiated to Carotid)
* Inv: Echo, if normal → Stress Echo
#### TTT 
* BB followed by CCB  
* surgery → septal ablation 
* arrhythmia → ICD
* HOCM + syncope → admission to hospital and ICD

### Ischemic heart disease

*Stable angina:* Chest pain <20 min - decreases with rest & GTN
TTT → short acting nitrate, BB, CCB, Aspirin 75 mg & statin

*Unstable angina:* New onset <24h or deterioration of stable angina
Chest pain >20min - less responsive to nitrates

*Inv*
1st → ECG → dynamic signs of MI reversible
2nd → if normal → stress ECG
3rd → Thallium scan → superior to stress ECG

*Mgmt* : 1- sublingual nitrate 2 - BB

### Myocardial infarction
*C/p: Sever > 30 min chest pain - May not respond to GTN*

1st step→ ECG → STEMI or NSTEMI
IF NSTEMI → 2nd step→ Cardiac enzymes → earliest → Myoglobin - sensitive → Troponin - detect reinfarction → CK-MB

*TTT*
① STEMI: ① loading Aspirin - Clopidogrel
         ② IV Heparin
         ③ Nitrates
           Coronary Angioplasty most imp step
- STEMI + tertiary hospital more than 2 hr → fibrinolytic
- Significant Bradycardia after MI → Atropine + temporary pacemaker
- Papillary muscle rupture after MI → Acute pulmonary edema

② NSTEMI: Normal cardiac enzymes, elevated cardiac enzymes.
           Repeat ECG & enzymes 3-6h → Aspirin - Clopidogrel
           IV heparin
           Nitrates
           Continuous Monitor 3-6h
