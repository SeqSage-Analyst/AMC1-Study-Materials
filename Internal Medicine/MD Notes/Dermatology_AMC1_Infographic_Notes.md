## 🔴 SKIN INFECTIONS - BACTERIAL

### Impetigo
- **Cause**: Staph or Strep
- **Key Feature**: Thin vesicles with HONEY-COLORED CRUSTING
- **Location**: Face, hands, neck, extremities
- **Tx**: Mupirocin (topical)
- **Note**: School exclusion 24hrs after starting antibiotics

### Folliculitis
- **Cause**: Staph
- **Key Feature**: Yellow pustules with HAIR SHAFT IN CENTER
- **Tx**: Topical antibiotics, good hygiene

### Furuncle (Boil)
- **Cause**: Staph
- **Key Feature**: 1-5cm red/tender nodule with pus
- **Tx**: Warm compress (simple) OR drainage + antibiotics (severe)

### Carbuncle
- **Cause**: Staph (DM = risk factor)
- **Location**: Nape of neck, back
- **Tx**: Drainage + antibiotics

### Cellulitis
- **Cause**: Strep pyogenes (most common), then Staph
- **Key Features**: FEVER, REDNESS, SWELLING, WARMTH, TENDERNESS
- **Onset**: 1-2 days after injury
- **Tx**: IV flucloxacillin IMMEDIATELY, elevate leg
- **Complication**: Of wound/trauma

### Erysipelas
- **Cause**: Strep pyogenes
- **Key Feature**: Well-defined SLIGHTLY RAISED PAINFUL lesion
- **Tx**: Antibiotics

### Acne
- **Cause**: Propionibacterium acnes
- **Lesions**: Papules, pustules, nodules, cysts
- **Locations**: Face, chest, back
- **Tx**:
  - 1st line: TOPICAL RETINOID
  - Antibiotics: Doxycycline, Clindamycin
  - Severe: Oral Isotretinoin (TERATOGENIC)
- **Note**: Doxycycline causes PHOTOTOXICITY

## 🟠 SKIN INFECTIONS - FUNGAL/PARASITIC

### Tinea (Fungal Infections)

| Condition                                | Key Features                                                      | Treatment                                                  |
| ---------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Tinea Pedis** (Athlete's Foot)         | White erosions, bad odor                                          | Miconazole cream, keep feet dry                            |
| **Tinea Cruris** (Jock Itch)             | Red/scaly rash inner thighs/inguinal, NOT scrotum                 | Topical antifungal                                         |
| **Tinea Versicolor (Malassezia furfur)** | Hypo/hyperpigmented macules, fine branny scaling, BACK/NECK/CHEST | Selenium sulphide, Zinc, systemic ketoconazole/fluconazole |
| **Tinea Corporis** (Ringworm)            | Well-defined ITCHY CIRCULAR patches with scaly borders            | Miconazole, ketoconazole                                   |
| **Onychomycosis** (Nail fungus)          | Yellow, thick, brittle nails                                      | Oral terbinafine, itraconazole                             |
| **Tinea Capitis** (Scalp ringworm)       | Scaly patches with hair loss, BLACK-DOT, KERION                   | **Griseofulvin - MAIN TREATMENT**                          |

### Parasitic Infections

**Pediculosis (Lice)**
- Grayish/black lice in head/body/pubic hair
- **Spread by**: Shared hats, towels, combs, bedding, clothing
- **Tx**: Permethrin

**Scabies**
- **Organism**: Female Sarcoptes scabiei
- **Primary Lesion**: BURROW
- **Location**: Between fingers, wrist, breast, genitalia
  - Adults: Back/soles SPARED
  - Children: Can occur anywhere
- **Sx**: NOCTURNAL ITCHING, family history
- **Tx**: 
  - Permethrin 5% cream (kids/pregnant)
  - Re-treat after 1-2 weeks
  -Wash all clothes in hot water
  - Treat ALL family members
- **If still itching after treatment**: Re-treat → then cortisone

## 🟣 SKIN INFECTIONS - VIRAL

### Herpes Simplex (HSV I/II)
- **Lesion**: Multiple VESICLES on ERYTHEMATOUS BASE
- **Sx**: Fever, lymph node enlargement, burning pain
- **Sites**: Lips (herpes labialis), Finger (herpetic whitlow), Genitalia
- **Type I**: More common
- **Type II**: More recurrence
- **Tx**: Oral acyclovir

**Herpes Encephalitis**
- **Site**: Temporal lobe
- **Sx**: Fever, headache, confusion, seizures
- **LP**: RBCs in CSF
- **Tx**: IV ACYCLOVIR
- **Side effect**: Renal failure → give excessive hydration

**Herpes in Pregnancy**
- **Clinical**: Painful ulceration (grouped vesicles)
- **Tx**: Oral acyclovir
- **CSection**: If ACTIVE LESION = MUST
- **Suppressive**: Acyclovir from 36 weeks
- **Lactation**: No breastfeeding with active infection
- **Differentiate primary vs recurrent**: Serology

**Resistant HSV**: Foscarnet

### Herpes Zoster (Shingles)
- **Cause**: Varicella zoster reactivation
- **Lesion**: Vesicles on red base in BAND-LIKE DISTRIBUTION (DERMATOMAL)
- **Sx**: Painful rash, prickly nerve pain
- **Tx**: ONLY within 72 hours - acyclovir, famciclovir, valacyclovir
- **After 72hrs**: Treat pain only (post-herpetic neuralgia) → TCA, Gabapentin, Pregabalin
- **Complications**: 
  - Eye involvement: Herpes ophthalmicus
  - Post-herpetic neuralgia

### Warts (Verruca)
- **Cause**: HPV
- **Genital warts**: Condyloma acuminata
- **Note**: 65% resolve SPONTANEOUSLY
- **Complication**: Cervical cancer
- **Tx**:
  - 1st line: Podophylin resin
  - 2nd line: Imiquimod
  - Others: laser, electrocautery, cryotherapy

### Molluscum Contagiosum
- **Cause**: Pox virus
- **Lesion**: Flesh-colored, DOME-SHAPED papules with CENTRAL UMBILICATION
- **Very contagious** - YES to others
- **School exclusion**: NO
- **Tx**: Usually NONE needed

## 🟢 ALLERGIC/INFLAMMATORY CONDITIONS

### Atopic Dermatitis
- **Type**: Hereditary disorder
- **Associations**: Asthma, allergic rhinitis, conjunctivitis, family history
- **Location**: Cheeks, face, trunk; CHILDREN: flexures (antecubital fossa)
- **Sx**: Dry, papular rash, SCRATCHING MAKES IT WORSE
- **Tx**: Good lotions, rehydrate skin

### Contact Dermatitis
- **Causes**: Nickel, soaps, perfumes, cosmetics, poison ivy/oak
- **Sx**: Papular, itchy rash
- **Tx**: Cortisone cream
- **Primary**: Occurs in anyone (strong alkali)
- **Allergic**: Only in genetically susceptible

### Urticaria (Hives)
- **Cause**: Histamine release (IgE)
- **Lesion**: Well-defined WHEELS (solid elevation with CENTRAL CLEARING)
- **Sx**: Extremely itchy, may cause ANGIOEDEMA
- **Tx**: Oral antihistamines - CETIRIZINE (DRUG OF CHOICE)

### Angioedema
- **Hereditary**: C1 esterase inhibitor deficiency
- **Acquired**: ACE inhibitors (most common drug cause)
- **Test**: RAST TEST
- **Sx**: Swelling of lips/around eyes
- **EMERGENCY**: Laryngeal swelling → IM ADRENALINE + intubation

### Psoriasis
- **Mechanism**: Increased epidermal turnover
- **Lesion**: Thick SILVERY SCALES loosely attached
- **Key Sign**: If removed → BLEEDING SPOTS (AUSPITZ SIGN)
- **Tx**:
  - 1st line: Local cortisone
  - 2nd line: Local vit D (Calcipotriol)
  - UV light, high-potency corticosteroids
  - Severe: Methotrexate

### Eczema Herpeticum
- **History**: Atopic dermatitis + vesicles on top
- **Cause**: Secondary HSV infection
- **Risk**: LIFE THREATENING
- **Tx**: Acyclovir

### Pityriasis Rosea
- **Cause**: HHV 6
- **Features**: 
  - Starts with large scaly pink patch (HERALD/MOTHER patch) on chest/back
  - Then "Christmas tree" pattern
- **DDx**: Frequently confused with ringworm
- **Tx**: Calamine lotion, topical cortisone

### Erythema Multiforme
- **Cause**: HSV
- **Lesion**: TARGET lesion
- **Types**: Minor (skin only) vs Major (Stevens-Johnson syndrome)
- **Tx**: Cortisone, antihistamine, SJS: admission + hydration

## 🟤 PIGMENT DISORDERS

### Vitiligo
- **Cause**: Destruction of melanocytes (AUTOIMMUNE)
- **Associations**: Type 1 DM, Hashimoto's, pernicious anemia
- **Tx**: Narrow band PUVA, steroid, punch graft, camouflage

| Type | Key Features | Treatment |
|------|--------------|-----------|
| **Telogen Effluvium** | Stress/post-partum, GOOD PROGNOSIS, regrows in 4-6 months | Usually none |
| **Alopecia Areata** | Family history, EXCLAMATION MARK hair, autoimmune | Intralesional/topical steroid |

## 🏠 BENIGN TUMORS & LESIONS

### Lipoma
- Benign fatty tumor
- **Tx**: Usually none

### Dermatofibroma
- **Cause**: Trauma, insect bite
- **Sx**: Sometimes itchy
- **Tx**: Usually NO treatment

### Pyogenic Granuloma
- **Risk**: Pregnancy, trauma
- **Tx**: Excision (1st line)

### Seborrheic Keratosis
- **Age**: Old patients
- **Location**: Head, neck, back, trunk
- **Appearance**: Stuck-on
- **Malig transformation**: NO
- **Tx**: Cosmetic removal

### Seborrheic Dermatitis
- **Age**: Kids
- **If adult**: Suspect HIV
- **Tx**: Antifungals, topical corticosteroids

### Rosacea
- **Demographics**: Females
- **Sx**: Flushing face, worsens with emotions/hot drinks
- **Tx**: Metronidazole

### Oral Leukoplakia
- **Cause**: Smoking
- **Risk**: PREMALIGNANT → tongue cancer
- **DDx**: Candida (bleeds when scraped)
- **Ix**: Biopsy
- **Tx**: Stop smoking

## ⚫ SKIN CANCERS (HIGH-YIELD)

### Comparison Table

| Feature | BCC | SCC | MELANOMA |
|---------|-----|-----|----------|
| **Commonality** | Most common skin cancer | 2nd most common | Less common, MOST DANGEROUS |
| **Malignancy** | Locally malignant, NO distant spread | Malignant, CAN METASTASIZE | MOST AGGRESSIVE, CAN METASTASIZE |
| **Risk Factor** | Sunlight | Radiation, sun, burn, chronic infection, Actinic keratosis, Leukoplakia | Sun exposure |
| **Site** | Face | Lower lip, tongue | - |
| **Appearance** | Pearly raised edge + telangiectasia, central ulcer (rodent ulcer) | Quickly growing keratotic lesion | Variable: size↑, pigmentation changes, ulceration, bleeding, pain |
| **Biopsy** | Shave (2mm margin) | Excisional | Excisional (2-3mm margin) |
| **Tx** | Surgery with margin | Surgery | Surgery ALL STAGES |
| **Note** | Re-excise if + margin | Lower lip/tongue = SCC | Re-excise if + margin, refer to plastic if face |

### Melanoma - Key Points
- **Most important prognostic factor**: DEPTH
- **Spread**: Local → Lymph → Distant (brain/liver)
- **Investigation**: Excisional biopsy (2mm margin)
- **Treatment**: Wide excision (2cm margin)
- **Stage III/IV**: Immunotherapy, chemotherapy, palliative
- **Prevention**: Avoid sun (10am-4pm), sunglasses, self-exam

### Dysplastic Nevi
- **Feature**: Irregular border on trunk
- **Management**: Suspicious → excision, follow-up, sun protection

### Pre-Cancerous Lesions
- **Bowen's Disease**: Slowly growing red patch/plaque → may become SCC
- **Actinic Keratosis**: Elderly, sun-exposed → SCC risk
- **Keratoacanthoma**: RAPID progression, premalignant → SCC → REMOVAL

### Mohs Surgery
- **Best for**: BCC/SCC on face or around eyes
- **Advantage**: Tissue-sparing

## 📋 MISCELLANEOUS

### Frostbite
- **Affected**: Exposed areas (toes, fingers, nose, cheeks, ears)
- **Late**: Cyanosis, gangrene, edema, no pain
- **Tx**: Warm compress, re-warm in 40-42°C water

### Striae
- **Cause**: Rapid tension (pregnancy, weight gain, steroids)
- **Tx**: L-ascorbic acid, isotretinoin, laser

### Candidal Infection
- **Risk**: DM, HIV, Pregnancy
- **Types**: Intertrigo, Napkin rash, Oral thrush, Vaginal
- **Tx**: Nystatin, fluconazole

## 🎯 KEY EXAM FLASH POINTS

1. **Scabies**: Very contagious STD, treat ALL family, nocturnal itching
2. **Herpes Encephalitis**: Temporal lobe, RBCs in CSF, IV acyclovir → renal failure side effect
3. **Herpes in Pregnancy**: Active lesion → C-section MUST
4. **Cellulitis**: IV fluoxacillin IMMEDIATELY, elevate leg
5. **Tinea Capitis**: Griseofulvin is MAIN TREATMENT
6. **Acne**: 1st line topical retinoid, isotretinoin = teratogenic
7. **Melanoma**: Depth = most important prognostic factor
8. **BCC**: Most common in Australia, locally malignant
9. **Urticaria**: Cetirizine is drug of choice
10. **Angioedema Emergency**: Laryngeal → IM adrenaline + intubation
11. **Psoriasis**: Auspitz sign = bleeding when scale removed
12. **Herpes Zoster**: Treat ONLY within 72 hours
13. **Molluscum**: School exclusion NO
14. **Warts**: 65% resolve spontaneously
