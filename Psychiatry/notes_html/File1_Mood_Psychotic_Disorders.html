🔀 MDD Treatment Algorithm (AMC/RANZCP 2020)
1st Line: CBT + SSRI
Mild-moderate → CBT alone may suffice | Severe → medication needed
Review at 2 weeks → No improvement yet?
⏳ Just CONTINUE — effect takes 2–6 weeks
Review at 6 weeks → Partial response?
STEP 1: Increase SSRI dose
Still insufficient?
STEP 2: Augment with Lithium or Antipsychotic
Review at 6 weeks → NO response at all?
Switch to another SSRI (1st) or SNRI (2nd)
Duloxetine / Venlafaxine
⚠️
Exam Trap — Before ANY antidepressant

Always ask: "Any previous history of Mania?" → Antidepressants can trigger mania in undiagnosed Bipolar

⚡ Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT)
✅ Indications
  • Severe / psychotic depression
  • Suicidal patient needing rapid response
  • Melancholia with severe malnutrition
  • Postpartum psychosis
  • Refractory depression
📋 Consent for ECT
  • Voluntary patient → Patient himself
  • Involuntary patient → Mental Health Tribunal
  • Minor (child) → Mental Health Tribunal
🎯
AMC Pearl

Key value of ECT = Gives IMMEDIATE response (unlike SSRIs which take 2–6 weeks)
Key side effect = Transient memory disturbance

Scenario: "Patient complains of memory problems after recent psychiatric admission?" → Ask: Did you receive ECT?

🚨 Suicide Risk — Must Know
⛔
Highest Risk Group
  • #1 risk factor: History of previous suicidal attempt
  • Has a specific plan
  • Single male
  • Physician / immigrant / recently unemployed
  • Early dementia
  • Recent discharge from hospital (highest risk = first 2 weeks post-discharge)
💬
Key Clinical Points
  • Most important question to ask depressed patient: "Do you have suicidal thoughts?"
  • Asking about suicide does NOT increase risk
  • Once identified → Involuntary admission immediately
  • Females attempt more; Males succeed more
  • Married = lowest risk
👴
Geriatric Depression Scale (GDS) Scoring

0–4 = Normal | 5–9 = Mild depression | 10–15 = More severe depression

🔀 Acute Mania — Management
INVOLUNTARY ADMISSION
1st: Antipsychotic (IMMEDIATE)
Risperidone / Aripiprazole / Quetiapine
2nd: Add Mood Stabilizer
Lithium OR Valproic Acid
🎯
Why antipsychotic FIRST?

Antimanic effects of mood stabilizers take time → antipsychotic gives immediate control

🔀 Bipolar Depression — Management
1st Line: Lithium (BEST)
OR Lamotrigine OR Valproic Acid
2nd Line: Antipsychotic
Best = Quetiapine
⚠️
Antidepressant-Induced Mania

1st: Stop antidepressant → 2nd: Give antipsychotic

🔀 Long-Term Bipolar Maintenance
1st Line: Lithium (Drug of choice for long-term)
Not controlled?
Add Valproic Acid OR Antipsychotic (Quetiapine / Aripiprazole)
On antipsychotic but still not controlled?
Add Lithium OR Valproic Acid
⏱
Minimum duration of treatment for Bipolar

At least 2 years

🎯 High-Yield Bipolar Scenarios
Q: Bipolar patient develops depression while on antipsychotic?
→ Continue antipsychotic AND ADD antidepressant
Q: Bipolar patient develops mania after starting antidepressant?
→ 1st STOP antidepressant → 2nd Give antipsychotic
Q: Most important question BEFORE starting antidepressant?
→ "Any previous history of Mania?"

⚖️ Positive vs Negative Symptoms
✅ Positive Symptoms (better prognosis)
  • Hallucinations (auditory most common)
  • Delusions
  • Disorganized speech/behavior
  • Respond better to antipsychotics
❌ Negative Symptoms (worse prognosis)
  • Flat affect
  • Alogia (poverty of speech)
  • Avolition (lack of motivation)
  • Social withdrawal
  • Anhedonia

AMC Pearls — Schizophrenia
  • Most affected cognitive function in schizophrenia = Proverb interpretation
  • Most common cause of death in schizophrenia = CVS disease, then suicide
  • People at highest risk of being hurt by schizophrenic patient = Parents
  • Drug of choice for Catatonic schizophrenia = Benzodiazepine
  • Brief Psychotic Disorder = Psychotic reaction to severe stress in a normal person (e.g., girlfriend left → delusions)

🚨
Most Important Questions
  • After any delivery: "How is your mood?"
  • If psychosis suspected: "Any thoughts of hurting yourself or baby?"
  • Most concerning symptom of postpartum psychosis: Delusions
🎯
Clinical Clues
  • Postpartum psychosis + Fever → Rule out infection
  • Postpartum psychosis + No fever → Do urine toxicology
  • Recurrence risk: psychosis 15–20%, depression 20–40%
  • Prophylactic antipsychotic during pregnancy = NOT recommended (give after delivery)
⏱
Timing Pearl

Highest timing to develop psychiatric disorder in pregnancy = Puerperium (postpartum period)
