Personality Disorders

🔴 HIGH PRIORITY
Cluster A — "Weird" (Odd/Eccentric)
Paranoid · Schizoid · Schizotypal

Social detachment, odd beliefs, restricted emotional expression. Think: resemblance to psychosis without meeting full psychotic criteria.

Cluster B — "Wild" (Dramatic/Emotional/Erratic)
Borderline · Antisocial · Narcissistic · Histrionic

Impulsivity, dramatic behavior, difficulty with emotions. Most commonly tested cluster in AMC.

Cluster C — "Worried" (Anxious/Fearful)
Avoidant · Dependent · Obsessive-Compulsive PD

Anxiety-driven behaviors, fear of rejection, need for control or others' support.

🎯 Critical Differentiators — AMC Exam Traps
Lives alone + No interest in any social relationships?
→ Schizoid
Same + Delusions or magical thinking?
→ Schizotypal
Lives alone + WANTS relationships but AFRAID + low self-confidence?
→ Avoidant
Female, multiple broken relationships, recurrent self-injury?
→ Borderline
Male, 20yo, multiple crimes, drugs, alcohol, aggression?
→ Antisocial PD (must be ≥18)
Same picture, but under 18?
→ Conduct Disorder
Patient: "You're the BEST doctor, my old GP was the WORST"?
→ Borderline = Splitting defense mechanism
Patient always late at work, perfect routine, cares about perfection?
→ OC Personality Disorder (NOT OCD — different!)
⚠️
Critical Distinction: OCD vs OC Personality Disorder

OCD = Ego-dystonic (patient is distressed, wants to stop obsessions) | OC Personality = Ego-syntonic (patient sees their perfectionism as reasonable)

⭐ Borderline Personality Disorder — Deep Dive
Diagnostic Features (≥5 of 9)
  • Frantic efforts to avoid abandonment
  • Unstable, intense relationships
  • Identity disturbance
  • Impulsivity (spending, sex, substance abuse)
  • Recurrent suicidal behavior / self-injury
  • Emotional instability (mood swings)
  • Chronic feelings of emptiness
  • Inappropriate, intense anger
  • Transient paranoid ideation
🎭
Key Defense Mechanism: SPLITTING

Seeing things as entirely all-good or all-bad — no middle ground. "Best doctor in the world" one day → "Worst doctor" the next.

💊
Treatment

First line: Dialectical Behavioral Therapy (DBT)
Also: Psychodynamic therapy

🎯
AMC Pearl

One of the most common patients to sue physicians for small things. Wrist = most common self-injury site.

Panic Disorder & Related Anxiety

🔴 HIGH PRIORITY
🫀 Panic Disorder — Clinical Features
  • Female in her 20s
  • Periods of intense fear/discomfort
  • Tachypnea, chest pain, palpitations, diaphoresis
  • Nausea, trembling, dizziness
  • Fear of dying or "going crazy"
  • Depersonalization
  • Perioral/acral paresthesias
  • Agoraphobia present in 30–50% of cases
🔬
VBG Finding

↑ pH (alkalosis) + ↓ CO₂ → Respiratory alkalosis from hyperventilation

🔀 Panic Disorder Treatment
1st Line: CBT
Graded Exposure + Relaxation techniques
Short-term: Beta-blocker OR Benzodiazepine
Good for acute/immediate relief
Long-term: SSRI
Best for chronic management
⚠️
Diet triggers

Caffeine and alcohol can trigger panic attacks

🪜 Gradual Exposure Ladder — AMC Favourite! (Supermarket Example)

Always start from the LEAST anxiety-provoking step and work UP gradually:

1️⃣ Writing up a shopping list — LOW anxiety (start here)
2️⃣ Getting in the car to go to the supermarket
3️⃣ Driving to the supermarket
4️⃣ Parking in the supermarket car park
5️⃣ Entering the supermarket
6️⃣ Walking through the supermarket aisles
7️⃣ Waiting at the supermarket checkout — HIGH anxiety (end goal)
🎯
Exam Rule

Always manage by starting from the LOWEST anxiety step — NEVER throw patient in at the deep end

🎯 Panic vs Agoraphobia — Key Distinction
Panic Disorder Only

Attacks occur WITHOUT a specific trigger

Agoraphobia Only

Attacks occur ONLY in specific situations (closed places, crowds)

Panic + Agoraphobia

Attacks occur BOTH with AND without triggers

🔁 OCD — Key Points
🧠
Clinical Features
  • Obsessions: persistent, unwanted, intrusive thoughts
  • Compulsions: repeated behaviors (washing, checking, counting)
  • Patient KNOWS thoughts are excessive and irrational
  • Patient WANTS to get rid of them (ego-dystonic)
  • Course: waxing and waning
💊
Treatment
  • Best psychotherapy: Exposure and Response Prevention
  • Drug of choice: SSRI
  • In children: Fluvoxamine
🎯
Hoarding Disorder

Excessive accumulation + distress when discarding → Treatment: SSRI

🎯 GAD vs Depression — Critical Exam Trap
30yo female, doesn't sleep well, worrying about kids, finances, husband's work?
→ GAD (worry about multiple life events, ≥6 months, ≥3 somatic symptoms, no full depression picture)
30yo female, excessive worries AND lost interest in life, not eating or sleeping?
→ Depression (Once you see Anhedonia + neurovegetative symptoms → say Depression, NOT GAD)
⚠️
Rule

If you see full depression criteria → diagnose Depression, even if there is concurrent anxiety. Long-term complication of GAD = Depression

📅 Adjustment Disorder
Key Features
  • Emotional/behavioral reaction to a stressful event
  • Response within 3 months of the event
  • Disproportionate to the stressor
  • Does NOT meet criteria for another disorder
Patient separated from boyfriend + full depression picture?
→ Diagnose as Depression, NOT Adjustment Disorder
(if full criteria met → always diagnose the primary disorder)

Childhood Psychiatric Disorders

🔴 HIGH PRIORITY
⚡ ADHD — Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder
📋
Key Facts
  • Age of onset: 5–7 years
  • Male > Female
  • Prognosis: Usually persists into adulthood
🔍
Three Core Features
  • Inattention: Can't concentrate, forgets instructions, switches tasks
  • Hyperactivity: Constant fidgeting, restless
  • Impulsivity: Acts without thinking, can't wait in line, can't follow orders/rules
💊
Management (STEP BY STEP)
  • 1st step: Call teacher → ask for school record (collect history)
  • Medication 1st line: Methylphenidate OR Dexamphetamine
  • Non-compliant/can't take at school: Give long-acting form
  • If adult requests Rx after long gap: Rule out abuse/addiction
⚠️
Differential Diagnosis

ADHD-like picture + Speech delay = Think DEAFNESS first! → Do hearing test

🎯
Tourette's Key Facts
  • Usually boys; Autosomal dominant genetics
  • Associations: ADHD (most common), OCD, learning disorders
  • Coprolalia (saying obscene words) = vocal tic
  • Course: wax and wane
  • Tics preceded by urge → tic → relief cycle
  • Patient CAN suppress briefly → followed by rebound
💊
Tourette's Treatment
  • 1st line: CBT + Clonidine (now preferred over antipsychotics)
  • 2nd line: Antipsychotics (Haloperidol)
  • Prognosis: usually lifelong

🎯
Key Differentiators
  • Aggressive child + history of revenge but NO crimes = ODD
  • Aggressive child even toward animals + Commit crimes = Conduct Disorder
  • Conduct disorder + 18yo = Antisocial Personality Disorder
🤫
Selective Mutism

Child unable to speak in certain social settings (e.g., school) BUT can speak at home where comfortable and safe. NOT a speech disorder — they CAN speak.
