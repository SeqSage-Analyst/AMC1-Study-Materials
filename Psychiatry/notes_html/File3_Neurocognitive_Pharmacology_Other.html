Dementia & Its Types

🔴🔴 TOP PRIORITY
🇦🇺
Australian Context — Must Know
  • #1 psychiatric disease increasing in incidence in Australia = Dementia
  • Dementia = 2nd most common cause of death in Australia
📋
Definition & Core Symptoms

Global cognitive impairment affecting memory + at least one of:
Aphasia · Apraxia · Agnosia · impaired executive function

📊 MMSE Scoring + Dementia Work-up
Score (out of 30)Severity
19–24Mild impairment
10–18Moderate impairment

Dementia Work-Up
  • Labs: TSH, RPR (syphilis), Folate, B12
  • Imaging: CT brain (brain atrophy)
  • If 1 test positive: Do another confirmatory test
  • 1st advice: Increase social + physical activities

⚡ Delirium (Acute Confusion) — Key Points
📋
Clinical Features
  • Acute onset — waxing and waning consciousness
  • Worse at night (sundowning)
  • Hallucinations, illusions, delusions
  • Disturbed sleep-wake cycle
  • Combative, anxious, paranoid, or stuporous
🔍
Common Causes
  • UTI — very common in elderly (occult UTI) → do urine analysis
  • New or multiple medications
  • Post-surgery
  • Acute medical condition
💊
Treatment
  • 1st: Non-pharmacological re-orientation
  • Violent patient: IM Haloperidol + physical restraints if needed
🎯
2 most common causes of agitation after hospital admission

1. Delirium → IM Haloperidol
2. Alcohol withdrawal → Diazepam

🚨
If patient attacks you at home

→ LEAVE IMMEDIATELY — do not engage

🔀 Agitated Patient — Management Algorithm
If in hospital/clinic → Call security FIRST
If at patient's home → LEAVE immediately
Then: Give Medication (IM)

Delirious patientAntipsychotic

Amphetamine / drug toxicityBenzodiazepine

Significantly agitatedIM Lorazepam

Psychopharmacology

💊 SSRIs — The Most Important Drug Class
SSRIs List
  • Fluoxetine
  • Sertraline (↑ suicide risk)
  • Escitalopram
  • Paroxetine
  • Citalopram
SSRI Side Effects
  • GI symptoms (most common side effect)
  • Sexual dysfunction (delayed ejaculation)
  • ↑ Suicidal risk (especially Sertraline)
🎯
SSRI Special Scenarios
  • GI side effects not tolerated → Switch to another SSRI
  • Sexual side effects → Reassure and wait (usually resolve spontaneously)
  • Erectile dysfunction → Can add Sildenafil
  • SSRI delayed ejaculation → Best Rx for premature ejaculation!
  • Nifedipine + Sertraline → Palpitations + hypotension (SSRI increases Nifedipine level)
🔄
How to Switch between SSRIs

1. Gradually stop old SSRI → 2. Washout period (few days) → 3. Gradually introduce new SSRI

Discontinuation Syndrome: Sudden stop → restart and taper over ≥2 weeks

⚠️ Tricyclic Antidepressants (TCAs)
TCA Names
  • Nortriptyline · Desipramine · Amitriptyline · Imipramine · Doxepin
Indications
  • Depression, anxiety
  • Chronic pain / fibromyalgia
  • Migraine prophylaxis
  • Enuresis (Imipramine)
⚠️
TCA Toxicity — Must Know
  • Risk: Arrhythmia (V.Tach)
  • ECG: QRS prolongation
  • Treatment: IV NaHCO₃ (ONLY if QRS prolonged)
Elderly brought to ED confused, empty bottle beside her, ECG shows prolonged QRS?
→ TCA Toxicity → Give IV NaHCO₃
TCA Side Effects (ANTI-ChE)

Anticholinergic: Dry mouth, constipation, urinary retention, sedation, diplopia, delirium
Also: Hyperprolactinemia, sexual problems

🧲 Lithium — First Line Mood Stabilizer
Indications
  • Mania (acute)
  • Bipolar (long-term prevention)
  • Best for bipolar depression
🎯
Routine Monitoring

Kidney function tests + Thyroid function tests

⚠️
Lithium Toxicity (Narrow Therapeutic Index)
  • Drugs precipitating toxicity: ACEi, NSAIDs, Thiazides
  • Most common symptom: Tremor
  • Cut-off level: 2.5 mmol/L
  • Treatment of choice: Dialysis
  • Fine tremors in patient on lithium → Give Beta-blocker
⚠️
Lithium Side Effects

Nephrogenic DI (thirst, polyuria) · Acne · Hypothyroidism (weight gain) · Nausea, diarrhea, seizures · Teratogenicity · Tremor (very common)

⚠️
Other Mood Stabilizers

Valproic acid: Mania + Bipolar; Teratogenic
Lamotrigine: Bipolar depression; Can cause severe rash — monitor closely

🍺 Alcohol — Australia-Specific Facts
Safe Drinking Guidelines (Australia)
  • Max: Not more than 4 standard drinks/day OR 10/week
  • Pregnant: NONE
  • Breastfeeding: NONE
  • Legal drinking age in Australia: 18 years
  • Most common cause of male infertility = Alcohol
  • #1 cause of teratogenicity in Australia = Alcohol
⚠️
Fetal Alcohol Syndrome

Main features: Absent philtrum + Thin upper lip
When baby grows up: Irritability and agitation

🎯
Key AMC Pearls — Alcohol
  • Most important question to detect alcoholism: "Eye opener?"
  • 1st step in ED for alcoholic patient: Check blood sugar
  • Reduce alcohol in Australia: Raise taxes
  • GP role to decrease alcoholism: Public lectures
🧠 Wernicke's Encephalopathy
⚠️
Classic Triad (from chronic alcoholism → Thiamine deficiency)
  • Ataxia + Nystagmus
  • Confusion
  • Confabulation
💊
Treatment ORDER is critical!

1. Check blood sugar → 2. Give Thiamine FIRST → 3. Then Glucose
⚠️ Never give glucose before thiamine — precipitates Wernicke's!

⛽
Petrol Sniffing (Australia-Specific)
  • Most commonly: Aboriginal people, age 12–15 years
  • Key symptom: Visual hallucinations
  • Risk: Aspiration pneumonitis
  • 1st step if hypoxic: ABG

🚨 Anorexia — Involuntary Admission Criteria
⛔
Admit Against Will If:
  • BMI < 14
  • Critical vitals (hypotension, bradycardia, hypothermia)
  • Severe hypokalemia
  • Suicidal ideation
🎯
AMC Pearls
  • Most urgent investigation = Electrolytes
  • Most important feature = BMI < 18.5
  • Anorexia + amenorrhea → 1st step = increase weight then decrease exercise
  • If pregnant with anorexia → Low birth weight
  • Gonadotropins NOT routine in anorexia
🎯 Eating Disorder Scenarios
Recurrent vomiting or exercise + LOW BMI?
→ Anorexia Nervosa
Recurrent vomiting or exercise + NORMAL BMI?
→ Bulimia Nervosa
Attack of excessive eating + induced vomiting?
→ Bulimia Nervosa
Attack of excessive eating + NO vomiting/laxatives?
→ Binge Eating Disorder
Young female, feeling body shape defective + exercise + NORMAL BMI?
→ Body Dysmorphic Disorder
Same + LOW BMI?
→ Anorexia Nervosa

Grief

🕊️ Grief vs Depression + Stages
5 Stages of Grief (Kübler-Ross)
  • Denial – "This can't be happening to me"
  • Anger – "Why is this happening?"
  • Bargaining – "Make this not happen and I will..."
  • Depression – "I'm too sad to do anything"
  • Acceptance – "I'm at peace with what happened"
🎯
Normal Grief Features
  • Hallucinations (visual or auditory) of the deceased = NORMAL
  • Complicated grief = symptoms lasting >6 months
  • Anniversary reaction = worsening around anniversary date
FeatureNormal GriefDepression

Intense guiltAbsentPresent

GP's Role in Grief + Treatment

  • If depression develops → SSRI
  • Severe insomnia → treat as insomnia
  • Aboriginal + grief + hallucination → Refer to Aboriginal counsellor

🪞 Dissociative Disorders
Dissociative Fugue
  • Sudden memory loss + loss of identity
  • Patient doesn't remember name/personal life
  • Assumes a new personality and name
  • Affects memory, identity, emotion, behavior, perception
Dissociative Identity Disorder
  • Multiple personalities controlling the person
  • Usually related to childhood sexual abuse

General Clinical Pearls
  • Most stressful psychological event = Marital separation
  • Wrist = most common site for self-injury
  • Most common reaction to parental divorce in kids = Behavior problems
  • What significantly increases survival in elderly = Living children
  • 1st step in treatment of any psychiatric patient = Establish good therapeutic relationship
  • Aboriginal patient + psychiatric illness = Refer to Aboriginal health care

Scenario-Based Pearls
  • Patient asks about your personal life → Boundaries violation
  • Sexual contact adult + minor → Report it
  • Hallucinations with antidepressant → Normal
  • Homeless psychotic patient: 1st step = Collateral history from police
  • Deja vu = Normal phenomenon, NOT a disease
  • Déjà vu vs Jamais vu: Déjà = seen before (but not); Jamais = never seen before (but have)
