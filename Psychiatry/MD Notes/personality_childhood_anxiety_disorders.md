## 🎭 Personality Disorders
**Priority: 🔴 HIGH PRIORITY**

### 📋 All Personality Disorders — Master Comparison

| Disorder                    | Cluster | Sex   | Key Features                                                                                                                 | Classic Scenario                                                                   | Treatment                            |
| :-------------------------- | :------ | :---- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :----------------------------------- |
| **Paranoid**                | A       | M=F   | Distrustful, suspicious, interprets others' actions as threatening, emotionally cold                                         | Patient convinced coworkers are plotting against him, no evidence                  | Psychotherapy                        |
| **Schizoid**                | A       | M>F   | Loner, detached, no interest in social relationships, no interest in sex or criticism, emotionally sufficient                | Lives alone, no relationships, no desire for them, content                         | Psychotherapy                        |
| **Schizotypal**             | A       | M=F   | Like schizoid + magical thinking, ideas of reference, paranoid ideation, odd appearance/behavior                             | Lives alone, no relationships + DELUSIONS or magical thinking                      | Low-dose antipsychotic               |
| **Borderline ⭐**            | B       | **F** | Unstable mood, splitting, impulsivity, unstable relationships, self-injury, emptiness, inappropriate anger                   | Multiple broken relationships, recurrent self-harm, "You're the best/worst doctor" | Dialectical Behavioral Therapy (DBT) |
| **Antisocial**              | B       | **M** | Recurrent criminal acts, ignores others' rights, impulsivity, aggression, no remorse, starts as conduct disorder (<18)       | 20yo male, multiple crimes, drug abuse, police involvement                         | CBT                                  |
| **Narcissistic**            | B       | M>F   | Grandiosity, preoccupation with success, lack of empathy, very sensitive to criticism                                        | Patient demands VIP room, feels superior to others                                 | Psychotherapy                        |
| **Histrionic**              | B       | **F** | Attention-seeking, seductive behavior, dramatic emotional expression, rapidly shifting emotions, excessive makeup/appearance | Over-dressed, sexually seductive, dramatic, needs to be center of attention        | Psychotherapy                        |
| **Avoidant**                | C       | F≥M   | Socially inhibited, rejection-sensitive, fears being disliked/ridiculed, lonely, very sensitive to criticism                 | Lives alone, WANTS relationships but is too afraid, low self-confidence            | CBT, SSRIs                           |
| **Dependent**               | C       | **F** | Needs to be taken care of, difficulty making decisions, fears abandonment, relies on husband/family                          | Common victim of abuse, cannot make any independent decision                       | Psychotherapy                        |
| **Obsessive-Compulsive PD** | C       | **F** | Perfectionism, preoccupied with order/control, inflexible morals, excessive detail at expense of efficiency                  | Always late to finish tasks due to need for perfection                             | Psychotherapy                        |

### 🎯 Critical Differentiators — AMC Exam Traps

> **Q: Lives alone + No interest in any social relationships?**
> A: **Schizoid**
> 
> **Q: Same + Delusions or magical thinking?**
> A: **Schizotypal**
> 
> **Q: Lives alone + WANTS relationships but AFRAID + low self-confidence?**
> A: **Avoidant**
> 
> **Q: Female, multiple broken relationships, recurrent self-injury?**
> A: **Borderline**

> **Q: Male, 20yo, multiple crimes, drugs, alcohol, aggression?**
> A: **Antisocial** PD (must be ≥18)
> 
> **Q: Same picture, but under 18?**
> A: **Conduct Disorder**
> 
> **Q: Patient: "You're the BEST doctor, my old GP was the WORST"?**
> A: **Borderline** = Splitting defense mechanism
> 
> **Q: Patient always late at work, perfect routine, cares about perfection?**
> A: **OC Personality Disorder** (NOT OCD — different!)

> ⚠️ **Critical Distinction: OCD vs OC Personality Disorder:** OCD = Ego-dystonic (patient is distressed, wants to stop obsessions) | OC Personality = Ego-syntonic (patient sees their perfectionism as reasonable)

### ⭐ Borderline Personality Disorder — Deep Dive

**Diagnostic Features (≥5 of 9)**
* Frantic efforts to avoid abandonment
* Unstable, intense relationships
* Identity disturbance
* Impulsivity (spending, sex, substance abuse)
* Recurrent suicidal behavior / self-injury
* Emotional instability (mood swings)
* Chronic feelings of emptiness
* Inappropriate, intense anger
* Transient paranoid ideation

> 🎭 **Key Defense Mechanism: SPLITTING:** Seeing things as entirely all-good or all-bad — no middle ground. "Best doctor in the world" one day → "Worst doctor" the next.
>
> 💊 **Treatment:** First line: **Dialectical Behavioral Therapy (DBT)**. Also: Psychodynamic therapy.
>
> 🎯 **AMC Pearl:** One of the most common patients to sue physicians for small things. Wrist = most common self-injury site.

## 😰 Panic Disorder & Related Anxiety
**Priority: 🔴 HIGH PRIORITY**

### 🫀 Panic Disorder — Clinical Features
* Female in her 20s
* Periods of intense fear/discomfort
* Tachypnea, chest pain, palpitations, diaphoresis
* Nausea, trembling, dizziness
* Fear of dying or "going crazy"

> 🔬 **VBG Finding:** ↑ pH (alkalosis) + ↓ CO₂ → Respiratory alkalosis from hyperventilation

### 🔀 Panic Disorder Treatment
1.  **1st Line: CBT** (Graded Exposure + Relaxation techniques)
2.  **Short-term: Beta-blocker OR Benzodiazepine** (Good for acute/immediate relief)
3.  **Long-term: SSRI** (Best for chronic management)

> ⚠️ **Diet triggers:** Caffeine and alcohol can trigger panic attacks

> 🎯 **Exam Rule:** Always manage by starting from the LOWEST anxiety step — NEVER throw patient in at the deep end.

### ⚖️ Anxiety Disorders — Master Comparison

| Disorder | Trigger | Duration | Key Feature | 1st Line Tx | Long-term Tx |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Panic Disorder** | No specific trigger (spontaneous) | Minutes (attacks) | Recurrent unpredictable attacks; physiological symptoms | CBT | SSRI |
| **Agoraphobia** | Specific situations (trapped/public places) | Minutes in trigger | Avoids public transport, open/closed spaces, crowds | CBT + Gradual exposure | SSRI |
| **Social Phobia** | Social/performance situations (e.g., public speaking) | Minutes | Fear of embarrassment; usually h/o school phobia | CBT | Beta-blocker for short-term, SSRI long-term |
| **Specific Phobia** | Specific object (animals, heights, planes) | Minutes | Circumscribed fear, knows it's irrational | Gradual exposure | CBT |
| **GAD** | Multiple/life situations | ≥6 months | ≥3 somatic symptoms (restlessness, fatigue, concentration, irritability, muscle tension, sleep disturbance) | CBT | SSRI |
| **OCD** | Obsessive thoughts → compulsive acts | Chronic (wax and wane) | Ego-dystonic; patient recognizes thoughts as own but irrational | Exposure + Response Prevention | SSRI (fluvoxamine in kids) |
| **PTSD** | Severe traumatic event | >1 month | Flashbacks (KEY WORD), avoidance, hyperarousal | CBT | SSRI |
| **Acute Stress Disorder** | Severe traumatic event | <1 month | Same as PTSD but shorter | CBT | — |
| **Adjustment Disorder** | Identifiable stressor | <3 months after stressor | Distress > expected; does NOT meet full MDD criteria | Supportive therapy | — |

### 🎯 Panic vs Agoraphobia — Key Distinction
* **Panic Disorder Only:** Attacks occur **WITHOUT a specific trigger**
* **Agoraphobia Only:** Attacks occur **ONLY in specific situations** (closed places, crowds)
* **Panic + Agoraphobia:** Attacks occur **BOTH with AND without** triggers

### 🔁 OCD — Key Points
* **Clinical Features:** Obsessions (intrusive thoughts), Compulsions (repeated behaviors). Patient KNOWS thoughts are excessive/irrational and WANTS to get rid of them (ego-dystonic). Waxing and waning course.
* **Treatment:** Best psychotherapy = **Exposure and Response Prevention**. Drug of choice = **SSRI** (Fluvoxamine in children).
* *Note on Hoarding Disorder:* Excessive accumulation + distress when discarding → Treatment: SSRI.

### 🎯 GAD vs Depression — Critical Exam Trap

> **Q: 30yo female, doesn't sleep well, worrying about kids, finances, husband's work?**
> A: **GAD** (worry about multiple life events, ≥6 months, ≥3 somatic symptoms, no full depression picture)
> 
> **Q: 30yo female, excessive worries AND lost interest in life, not eating or sleeping?**
> A: **Depression** (Once you see Anhedonia + neurovegetative symptoms → say Depression, NOT GAD)

> ⚠️ **Rule:** If you see full depression criteria → diagnose Depression, even if there is concurrent anxiety. **Long-term complication of GAD = Depression**.

## 👶 Childhood Psychiatric Disorders
**Priority: 🔴 HIGH PRIORITY**

### ⚡ ADHD — Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder
* **Key Facts:** Age of onset: **5–7 years**. Male > Female. Prognosis: Usually persists into adulthood.
* **Core Features:** * **Inattention:** Can't concentrate, forgets instructions.
    * **Hyperactivity:** Constant fidgeting.
    * **Impulsivity:** Acts without thinking, can't follow rules.
* **Management:** 1st step = Call teacher / ask for school record. 1st line med = **Methylphenidate OR Dexamphetamine**. Give long-acting form if non-compliant at school.
* ⚠️ **Differential Diagnosis:** ADHD-like picture + Speech delay = **Think DEAFNESS first!** → Do hearing test.

### ⚖️ Autism vs Asperger's Syndrome

| Feature | Autism | Asperger's Syndrome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Age of onset** | Before 3 years | Usually later recognition |
| **Language delay** | **Yes — prominent** | No — language normal |
| **Cognitive delay**| Often present (low IQ) | No — IQ normal |
| **Social impairment**| Severe (no eye contact, no friends, no play) | Present but milder |
| **Repetitive behavior**| Yes — fascination with toys, routine | Yes |
| **Communication** | Severely impaired verbal + non-verbal | Primarily social impairment |
| **Most important criteria** | Repetitive behavior + delayed speech + poor communication | Social impairment without language delay |

### 🔄 Tic Disorders — Comparison

| Condition | Tics | Duration | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Transient Tic Disorder** | Motor OR vocal (not both) | <12 months | CBT, reassurance |
| **Chronic Tic Disorder** | Motor OR vocal (not both) | >12 months | CBT, Clonidine |
| **Tourette's Syndrome** | **Motor AND vocal (both)** | Lifelong | CBT, **Clonidine (1st line)**, Antipsychotics |

> 🎯 **Tourette's Key Facts:** Usually boys; Autosomal dominant. Associated with ADHD (most common) and OCD. Coprolalia (saying obscene words) is a vocal tic. Patient CAN suppress briefly → followed by rebound.

### ⚖️ Oppositional Defiant Disorder vs Conduct Disorder

| Feature | ODD | Conduct Disorder |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Crimes / law breaking**| No crimes | **Yes — crimes, truancy, vandalism** |
| **Violence towards animals**| No | **Yes** |
| **Behavior** | Temper tantrums, argumentative, angry, spiteful, vindictive | Bullying, weapons, forced sex, fire-setting, stealing, drugs |
| **Age** | Any (school age) | Any (<18) |
| **Long-term outcome** | May resolve or progress | **Antisocial Personality Disorder (≥18)** |

> 🤫 **Selective Mutism:** Child unable to speak in certain social settings (e.g., school) BUT can speak at home where comfortable and safe. NOT a speech disorder — they CAN speak.

### 🎯 Childhood Disorder — Quick Scenario Reference

| Clinical Scenario | Diagnosis |
| :--- | :--- |
| 5yo boy, can't sit still, no concentration, poor grades, impulsive at school AND home | ADHD |
| Same picture + speech delay | Rule out Deafness first |
| 2yo, no eye contact, no speech, repetitive with toys, no friends, outburst if routine changes | Autism |
| Same but normal IQ, normal speech, just socially impaired | Asperger's |
| Boy with eye blinking + throat grunting (both motor AND vocal), >1 year | Tourette's Syndrome |
| Child only eye blinking, no vocal tics, >1 year | Chronic Motor Tic Disorder |
| 14yo, multiple crimes, drugs, alcohol, aggression, cruelty to animals | Conduct Disorder |
| Same picture, 20yo | Antisocial Personality Disorder |
| Child argues, has tantrums, resentful, spiteful — no crimes, no cruelty to animals | ODD |
| Child speaks normally at home but completely silent at school | Selective Mutism |
