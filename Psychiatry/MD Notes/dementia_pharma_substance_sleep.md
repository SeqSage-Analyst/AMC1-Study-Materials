## 🧠 Dementia & Its Types
**Priority: 🔴🔴 TOP PRIORITY**

### Definition & Australian Context
* **Definition:** Global cognitive impairment affecting memory + at least one of: **A**phasia, **A**praxia, **A**gnosia, impaired executive function.
* **Australian Context:** Dementia is the #1 psychiatric disease increasing in incidence and the **2nd most common cause of death** in Australia.

### 📊 MMSE Scoring + Dementia Work-up

**MMSE Scoring (out of 30):**
* 19–24 = Mild impairment
* 10–18 = Moderate impairment
* ≤9 = Severe impairment

**Dementia Work-Up:**
* **Labs:** TSH, RPR (syphilis), Folate, B12
* **Imaging:** CT brain (brain atrophy)
* **Rule:** If 1 test positive → Do another confirmatory test. 
* **1st advice:** Increase social + physical activities.

### ⭐ Dementia Types — Master Comparison (The Most Tested Table)

| Type | Frequency | Key Features | Pathology | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Alzheimer's Disease** | 50% (most common) | **Memory first affected (early)**. Face recognition = last affected. Insidious onset. Risk factors: Female, family h/o, head trauma, Down syndrome. | CT: diffuse cortical atrophy. ↓ ACh (cholinergic deficit) | **Donepezil** (cholinesterase inhibitor) OR **Memantine** (best if cardiac/bradycardia). + increase social/physical activity. |
| **Vascular Dementia** | 25% (2nd most common) | **H/o stroke or vascular risk factors**. **Focal neurological deficits**. Stepwise progression. | CT: Multiple infarcts | Control vascular risk factors (BP, glucose), antiplatelets. |
| **Lewy Body Dementia** | ~15% | Dementia + **Parkinsonism** + **Visual Hallucinations** (KEY WORD) | Lewy body inclusions in cortex | Cholinesterase inhibitors. ⚠️ Avoid typical antipsychotics (severe sensitivity). |
| **Frontal Lobe Dementia (Pick's Disease)** | ~5% | Dementia + **Sudden, significant personality change**. Very strange behavior, disinhibition. | Frontotemporal atrophy | Supportive; SSRIs for behavioral symptoms. |
| **Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus (NPH)**| Rare but important | Classic Triad: **Dementia + Urinary incontinence + Gait ataxia** (Wet, Wobbly, Wacky) | ↓ CSF drainage → Enlarged ventricles | **SHUNT surgery (treatable!)** |
| **Huntington's Disease** | Rare | Autosomal dominant. Classic Triad: **Dementia + Personality changes + Chorea**. Onset: 40s. | Caudate nucleus atrophy | Symptomatic. ⚠️ Do NOT test minors unless very high clinical suspicion. |

### 🎯 Dementia Type — Instant Identifier

| Clinical Clue | Diagnosis |
| :--- | :--- |
| Dementia + Visual hallucinations | **Lewy Body Dementia** |
| Dementia + Urinary incontinence + Gait ataxia | **Normal Pressure Hydrocephalus** |
| Dementia + After stroke / Focal deficits | **Vascular Dementia** |
| Dementia + Sudden significant personality change | **Frontal Lobe (Pick's) Dementia** |
| Dementia + Chorea + Autosomal dominant | **Huntington's Disease** |
| Dementia with NONE of the above — gradual onset | **Alzheimer's Disease** |
| Apparent dementia + Marked improvement with antidepressants | **Pseudodementia = Depression** |

### ⚖️ Dementia vs Delirium vs Normal Aging vs Pseudodementia

| Feature | Dementia | Delirium | Normal Aging | Pseudodementia (Depression) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset** | Insidious, gradual | **Acute** | Very gradual | Related to depressive episode |
| **Consciousness**| Clear (early) | Fluctuating, waxing/waning | Clear | Clear |
| **Course** | Progressive | Fluctuating (worse at night) | Stable | Improves with antidepressants |
| **Hallucinations**| Sometimes (late) | Common | Absent | Rare |
| **Reversible?** | No (mostly) | **Yes** (if cause treated) | N/A | **Yes** |
| **Daily function**| Impaired | Impaired | **NORMAL — lives independently** | Impaired |

> 🎯 **HRT Pearl:** HRT (Hormone Replacement Therapy) decreases incidence of Alzheimer's.

### ⚡ Delirium (Acute Confusion) — Key Points

* **Clinical Features:** Acute onset, waxing/waning consciousness. Worse at night (sundowning). Hallucinations, disturbed sleep-wake cycle.
* **Common Causes:** **UTI** (very common in elderly), new/multiple meds, post-surgery, acute medical condition.
* **Treatment:** 1st = Non-pharmacological re-orientation. Violent patient = IM Haloperidol. 
* ⚠️ **Safety Trap:** If a patient attacks you at home → **LEAVE IMMEDIATELY** — do not engage.

### 🔀 Agitated Patient — Management Algorithm
1.  **If in hospital/clinic:** Call security FIRST (If at patient's home → LEAVE immediately).
2.  **Then:** Give Medication (IM).

| Patient Type | Drug of Choice |
| :--- | :--- |
| Psychotic patient / Delirious patient / Post-operative | Antipsychotic (e.g., IM Haloperidol) |
| Mentally retarded child | Risperidone |
| Amphetamine / drug toxicity | Benzodiazepine |
| Alcohol intoxication/withdrawal | Diazepam (oral) |
| Alcohol + liver impairment | Lorazepam (no liver metabolism) |
| Significantly agitated | IM Lorazepam |

## 💊 Psychopharmacology

### 💊 SSRIs — The Most Important Drug Class
* **Drugs:** Fluoxetine, Sertraline (↑ suicide risk), Escitalopram, Paroxetine, Citalopram.
* **Side Effects:** GI symptoms (most common), sexual dysfunction, ↑ suicidal risk.
* **Switching SSRIs:** Gradually stop old → Washout period → Gradually introduce new.
* *Note:* SSRI delayed ejaculation is the best Rx for premature ejaculation.

### ⭐ Serotonin Syndrome vs Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome (NMS)

| Feature | Serotonin Syndrome | NMS (Neuroleptic Malignant Syndrome) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cause** | SSRI + MDMA/LSD/cocaine/amphetamine/MAOI | Antipsychotics (typical) |
| **Key Word** | **Hypertonia + Hyperreflexia** | **High fever + Muscle RIGIDITY** |
| **Fever** | Present | Present (high grade) |
| **Lab findings** | HTN, Tachycardia | **Elevated CK**, Autonomic instability |
| **Treatment** | Stop offending drug + BZD for agitation | Stop antipsychotic + **Dantrolene OR Bromocriptine** |

> 🎯 **Classic AMC Scenario:** Young male on SSRI went to party (MDMA) → confused, tachycardic, sweating = **Serotonin Syndrome**.

### ⚠️ Tricyclic Antidepressants (TCAs)
* **Drugs:** Nortriptyline, Desipramine, Amitriptyline, Imipramine, Doxepin.
* **Toxicity:** Risk of Arrhythmia (V.Tach). ECG shows **QRS prolongation**. Treatment = **IV NaHCO₃**.
* **Side Effects:** Anticholinergic (Dry mouth, constipation, urinary retention, sedation).

### 🧲 Lithium — First Line Mood Stabilizer
* **Indications:** Mania, Bipolar prevention, Bipolar depression.
* **Monitoring:** Kidney function tests + Thyroid function tests.
* **Toxicity:** Precipitated by **ACEi, NSAIDs, Thiazides**. Most common symptom = Tremor. Cut-off = 2.5 mmol/L. Treatment = Dialysis.
* *Other Mood Stabilizers:* Valproic acid (teratogenic), Lamotrigine (watch for severe rash).

### 🔬 Antipsychotics — Typical vs Atypical

| Feature | Typical (1st Gen) | Atypical (2nd Gen) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Examples** | Haloperidol, Chlorpromazine | Clozapine, Risperidone, Olanzapine, Quetiapine |
| **EPS risk** | **High** | **Lower** |
| **Metabolic effects**| Less | More (weight gain, DM, HLD) |
| **Prolactin elevation**| Yes | Some (Risperidone = most) |

**Extrapyramidal Side Effects (EPS):**
1.  **NMS:** High fever + rigidity. Tx: Dantrolene.
2.  **Acute Dystonia:** Muscle spasms. Tx: Benztropine or Diphenhydramine.
3.  **Akathisia:** Restlessness. Tx: Decrease dose + Beta-blockers/Diazepam.
4.  **Tardive Dyskinesia:** Involuntary face movements. Tx: Discontinue meds.
5.  **Pseudoparkinsonism:** Shuffling gait. Tx: Reduce dose.

**Atypical Antipsychotics — Specific SE:**
* **Clozapine:** Agranulocytosis (Monitor CBC).
* **Olanzapine:** Marked sedation, weight gain, DM.
* **Risperidone:** Hyperprolactinemia.

> 🧀 **MAOIs:** Hypertensive crisis if taken with high-tyramine foods (cheese, red wine). Monitor Blood Pressure.

## 🧪 Substance Abuse

### ⚖️ Drug Intoxication & Withdrawal

| Drug | Intoxication Features | Withdrawal Features | Key Antidote / Tx |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Opioids** | Respiratory depression, **Pinpoint pupils** | Yawning, diaphoresis, dilated pupils, N/V/D | **Naloxone** |
| **Amphetamines** | Agitation, HTN, **Dilated pupils**, **Psychosis** | Paranoia, depression | Benzodiazepine (for psychosis) |
| **Cocaine** | Agitation, dilated pupils, **Tactile hallucinations** | Depression, "crash" | Benzodiazepine |
| **PCP** | Same + **Vertical/horizontal nystagmus** | — | Benzodiazepine |
| **LSD** | Same + **Significant visual hallucinations** | — | Benzodiazepine |
| **Cannabis** | Euphoria, ↑appetite, **Conjunctival injection** | Irritability | Stop cannabis |
| **Alcohol** | Disinhibition, ataxia, slurred speech | Tremors, agitation, **Delirium Tremens (day 5)** | Benzodiazepine (Diazepam oral) |

### 🍺 Alcohol & Wernicke's Encephalopathy
* **Australia Facts:** Max 4 standard drinks/day or 10/week. Pregnant = NONE. Fetal Alcohol Syndrome = Absent philtrum + thin upper lip.
* **Wernicke's Encephalopathy Triad:** Ataxia + Nystagmus, Confusion, Confabulation.
* **Treatment Order:** 1. Check blood sugar → 2. **Give Thiamine FIRST** → 3. Then Glucose. (Never glucose before thiamine).

## 🍽️ Eating Disorders

| Feature | Anorexia Nervosa | Bulimia Nervosa | Binge Eating Disorder |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **BMI** | **Below 18.5** | **Normal or High** | **Normal or High** |
| **Compensatory behavior**| Yes (vomiting, laxatives, exercise) | Yes | None |
| **Treatment** | Weight restoration 1st, Fluoxetine, CBT | CBT + Fluoxetine | CBT |

> ⛔ **Anorexia Involuntary Admission:** Admit if BMI < 14, critical vitals, severe hypokalemia, or suicidal ideation.

## 🏥 Somatoform Disorders

| Condition | Mechanism | Key Features | Treatment |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Somatization** | Unconscious | Multiple chronic symptoms from different systems, normal workup | CBT, single regular physician |
| **Conversion** | Unconscious | Sudden severe stress → sudden neurologic symptom. *La belle indifférence* | Psychotherapy |
| **Hypochondriasis**| Unconscious | Fear of serious disease despite reassurance | Psychotherapy |
| **Factitious (Munchausen's)**| Conscious (no external gain) | Deliberately produces illness | Confront carefully |
| **Munchausen by Proxy** | Conscious | Fabricates illness in child | **REPORT (Child abuse)** |
| **Malingering** | Conscious (**external gain**) | Faking for money, avoiding work, drugs | Suspect and investigate |

## 😢 Grief

* **5 Stages (Kübler-Ross):** Denial, Anger, Bargaining, Depression, Acceptance.
* **Normal Grief vs Depression:** Hallucinations of the deceased are NORMAL. If it lasts >6 months = Complicated grief. If there is intense guilt, suicidal thoughts, or hopelessness = Depression (treat with SSRIs).

## ⚡ Impulse Control, Dissociative & Other Disorders

* **Kleptomania:** Steals unneeded objects. Anxiety before → Relief after. Specific Tx = Aversion therapy.
* **Pyromania:** Fire-setting. Anxiety before → Gratification after.
* **Trichotillomania:** Pulls own hair.
* **Dissociative Fugue:** Sudden memory loss + assumes new identity.
* **Dissociative Identity Disorder:** Multiple personalities, usually from childhood abuse.

> 🎯 **Malingering Trap:** Suspect malingering in illegal immigrants with psychological symptoms, prisoners with normal exams, or patients requesting narcotics for unverifiable pain.
