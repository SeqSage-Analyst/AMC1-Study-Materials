
## 😔 Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)
**Priority: 🔴🔴 TOP PRIORITY**

### 🧠 SIG E CAPS — Diagnostic Criteria
*(≥5 of 9 for ≥2 weeks, must include ① or ②)*

* **S** - Sleep disturbance *(insomnia or hypersomnia) — Early awakening = highly suggestive*
* **I** - Interest loss *① = Anhedonia — loss of all pleasant activities*
* **G** - Guilt *(worthlessness, inappropriate guilt)*
* **E** - Energy ↓ *(fatigue)*
* **C** - Concentration ↓
* **A** - Appetite ↓ *② = Depressed mood*
* **P** - Psychomotor agitation or retardation
* **S** - Suicidal ideation

### ⏱ Critical Timings

| Feature | Duration |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Diagnosis requires** | ≥ 2 weeks |
| **SSRI effect onset** | 2–6 weeks |
| **Treatment duration** | 6–12 months |
| **Medication taper** | ≥ 2 weeks |
| **Untreated episode** | 6–18 months (avg 8) |
| **Recurrence after Rx stop** | 35% |

### 📅 Seasonal Patterns
> **❄️ Most common season for Depression:** Winter
> 
> **🌸 Most common season for Suicide:** Spring
> 
> **🎯 Most important thing to check:** Mood (when you suspect depression)

### Depression Subtypes — Quick Comparison

| Subtype | Key Feature | Duration | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **MDD** | Full criteria (≥5 sx) | ≥2 weeks | Baseline diagnosis |
| **Dysthymic Disorder** | Chronic low-grade depression | ≥2 years | Never symptom-free >2 months |
| **Seasonal Affective (SAD)** | Depression only in winter | Winter months | Light therapy |
| **Atypical Depression** | ↑ Appetite + ↑ Sleep + ↑ Weight | ≥2 weeks | Mood reactive to +ve events |
| **Melancholic Depression** | Refusal to eat/drink, psychotic features | Severe | **1st line = ECT** |
| **Cyclothymic Disorder** | Depression + episodes of hypomania | Recurrent | Milder than Bipolar II |
| **Pseudodementia** | Apparent dementia that responds to antidepressants | Variable | = Depression in disguise |

### 🔀 MDD Treatment Algorithm (AMC/RANZCP 2020)

1.  **1st Line: CBT + SSRI** (Mild-moderate → CBT alone may suffice | Severe → medication needed)
    * *Review at 2 weeks → No improvement yet?*
2.  **⏳ Just CONTINUE** — effect takes 2–6 weeks
    * *Review at 6 weeks → Partial response?*
3.  **STEP 1: Increase SSRI dose**
    * *Still insufficient?*
4.  **STEP 2: Augment with Lithium or Antipsychotic**
    * *Review at 6 weeks → NO response at all?*
5.  **Switch to another SSRI (1st) or SNRI (2nd)** (Duloxetine / Venlafaxine)

> ⚠️ **Exam Trap — Before ANY antidepressant:** Always ask: **"Any previous history of Mania?"** → Antidepressants can trigger mania in undiagnosed Bipolar.

### 🎯 Special Scenarios — "Which Antidepressant?"

| Clinical Scenario | Preferred Antidepressant | Why |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard MDD (1st choice)** | SSRI | Best efficacy/tolerability profile |
| **Depression + Pregnancy** | Fluoxetine | Most studied in pregnancy |
| **Depression + Post-stroke** | Fluoxetine | Preferred after stroke |
| **Depression + Anorexia Nervosa** | Fluoxetine | Effective for mood + eating disorder |
| **Depression + Smoking cessation** | Bupropion | Treats both depression and nicotine dependence |
| **Depression + Insomnia** | Mirtazapine | Sedating → helps sleep |
| **Depression + Decreased appetite** | Mirtazapine | Increases appetite |
| **Depression + Cardiac patient (Alzheimer's)** | Memantine | Safer in cardiac patients (donepezil causes bradycardia) |

### ⚡ Electroconvulsive Therapy (ECT)

**✅ Indications**
* Severe / psychotic depression
* Suicidal patient needing rapid response
* Melancholia with severe malnutrition
* Postpartum psychosis
* Refractory depression

**📋 Consent for ECT**
* **Voluntary patient** → Patient himself
* **Involuntary patient** → Mental Health Tribunal
* **Minor (child)** → Mental Health Tribunal

> 🎯 **AMC Pearl:**
> * Key value of ECT = **Gives IMMEDIATE response** (unlike SSRIs which take 2–6 weeks)
> * Key side effect = **Transient memory disturbance**
> * *Scenario:* "Patient complains of memory problems after recent psychiatric admission?" → Ask: **Did you receive ECT?**

### 📅 Duration of Treatment Decision

| Duration                   | Indication                                                           |
| :------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Standard (6–12 months)** | First uncomplicated episode of MDD                                   |
| **3–5 years**              | One severe episode OR 2+ episodes OR family h/o recurrent depression |
| **Lifelong (debated)**     | Recurrent suicidal patients                                          |

## 🔄 Bipolar Disorder
**Priority: 🔴 HIGH PRIORITY**

### 🧠 DIG FAST — Symptoms of Mania

* **D** - Distractibility
* **I** - Insomnia *(no need for sleep — not tired)*
* **G** - Grandiosity *(elevated self-esteem)*
* **F** - Flight of ideas
* **A** - Activity ↑ *(psychomotor agitation + sexual indiscretions)*
* **S** - Speech ↑ *(talkativeness, pressured speech)*
* **T** - Thoughtlessness *(reckless behavior)*

### ⚖️ Mania vs Hypomania — Key Differences

| Feature | Mania | Hypomania |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Functional Impairment** | Marked impairment | No marked impairment |
| **Hospitalization needed**| Yes | No |
| **Psychosis** | Can occur | Absent |
| **Agitation** | Significant | Minimal |
| **Symptoms** | Same DIG FAST | Same but milder |

### 🔀 Acute Mania — Management
1.  **INVOLUNTARY ADMISSION**
2.  **1st: Antipsychotic (IMMEDIATE)** (Risperidone / Aripiprazole / Quetiapine)
3.  **2nd: Add Mood Stabilizer** (Lithium OR Valproic Acid)

> 🎯 **Why antipsychotic FIRST?** Antimanic effects of mood stabilizers take time → antipsychotic gives immediate control.

### 🔀 Bipolar Depression — Management
1.  **1st Line: Lithium (BEST)** (OR Lamotrigine OR Valproic Acid)
2.  **2nd Line: Antipsychotic** (Best = Quetiapine)

> ⚠️ **Antidepressant-Induced Mania:** 1st: Stop antidepressant → 2nd: Give antipsychotic.

### 🔀 Long-Term Bipolar Maintenance
1.  **1st Line: Lithium** (Drug of choice for long-term)
    * *Not controlled?*
2.  **Add Valproic Acid OR Antipsychotic** (Quetiapine / Aripiprazole)
    * *On antipsychotic but still not controlled?*
3.  **Add Lithium OR Valproic Acid**

> ⏱ **Minimum duration of treatment for Bipolar:** At least **2 years**.

### 🎯 High-Yield Bipolar Scenarios
* **Q: Bipolar patient develops depression while on antipsychotic?**
    * A: Continue antipsychotic AND ADD antidepressant
* **Q: Bipolar patient develops mania after starting antidepressant?**
    * A: 1st STOP antidepressant → 2nd Give antipsychotic
* **Q: Most important question BEFORE starting antidepressant?**
    * A: **"Any previous history of Mania?"**

## 🧩 Schizophrenia
**Priority: 🔴 HIGH PRIORITY**

### 📊 Risk Probability by Relationship

| Relationship | Risk |
| :--- | :--- |
| **General population** | 1% |
| **First-degree relative** | 12% |
| **Grandchildren** | 6% |
| **Single parent affected**| 12% |
| **Both parents affected** | 40% |
| **Monozygotic twins** | **47%** |
| **Dizygotic twins** | 12% |

### 🏥 Diagnostic Duration Rule

| Duration of Psychosis | Diagnosis |
| :--- | :--- |
| **< 1 month** | Brief Psychotic Disorder |
| **1–6 months** | Schizophreniform Disorder |
| **> 6 months** | **Schizophrenia** |

> 🎯 **Age of onset:** 18–25 years → High school / college students. Drop of grades + isolation = **Prodrome of schizophrenia until proved otherwise**.

### 📋 Schizophrenia Types

| Type | Key Feature | Prognosis |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Paranoid** | Delusions and/or hallucinations (+ symptoms dominant)| Better |
| **Disorganized** | Disordered speech and behavior | Moderate |
| **Catatonic** | Immobility, mutism, waxy flexibility, echolalia/echopraxia | Catatonic = Good prognostic sign |

### ⚖️ Positive vs Negative Symptoms

**✅ Positive Symptoms (better prognosis)**
* Hallucinations (auditory most common)
* Delusions
* Disorganized speech/behavior
* Respond better to antipsychotics

**❌ Negative Symptoms (worse prognosis)**
* Flat affect
* Alogia (poverty of speech)
* Avolition (lack of motivation)
* Social withdrawal
* Anhedonia

### 🔀 Schizophrenia Management Algorithm

1.  **1st Line: Atypical Antipsychotic** (Risperidone / Quetiapine / Aripiprazole)
    * *No effect after 3 weeks?*
2.  **Increase the dose**
    * *Still no effect?*
3.  **Switch to another antipsychotic**
    * *All antipsychotics failed?*
4.  **LAST RESORT: Clozapine** (⚠️ Never start with clozapine — agranulocytosis risk)

> 🎯 **Non-compliant patient?** → Give Long-acting (Depot) Risperidone
> ⏱ **Minimum treatment duration** → 2 years minimum

### 📖 Schizophrenia-Related Terminology

| Term | Definition | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Circumstantiality** | Excessive details but DOES eventually answer | Gets there in the end |
| **Tangentiality** | Irrelevant answers, NEVER answers the question | Never reaches the point |
| **Derailment (loose association)** | Jumping from one unrelated idea to another | Hallmark of thought disorder |
| **Delusion** | False, fixed belief | Antipsychotic = preferred Rx |
| **Impaired Insight** | Very important sign in schizophrenia | Patient doesn't know they're ill |
| **Folie à deux** | Shared delusion within family members | Transmitted from one to another |
| **Capgras Syndrome** | Belief that loved one replaced by identical impostor | Type of delusion |
| **Overvalued Idea** | Unreasonable but accepted idea, not a delusion | Patient accepts it as their own |

> 🎯 **AMC Pearls — Schizophrenia**
> * Most affected cognitive function in schizophrenia = **Proverb interpretation**
> * Most common cause of death in schizophrenia = **CVS disease**, then suicide
> * People at highest risk of being hurt by schizophrenic patient = **Parents**
> * Drug of choice for Catatonic schizophrenia = **Benzodiazepine**
> * Brief Psychotic Disorder = Psychotic reaction to severe stress in a normal person (e.g., girlfriend left → delusions)

## 🤱 Postpartum Psychiatric Disorders

### ⚖️ Postpartum Blues vs Depression vs Psychosis — Master Comparison

| Feature | Baby Blues | Postpartum Depression | Postpartum Psychosis |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Onset** | Days 1–5 | Within 4 weeks | Within 2 weeks (1st 48h peak) |
| **Duration** | <2 weeks | Weeks–months | Acute |
| **Frequency** | 50–80% of mothers | 10–15% | 0.1–0.2% |
| **Key features** | Mood swings, tearfulness, no depressive criteria | Full MDD criteria | **Delusions**, hallucinations, confusion |
| **Treatment** | Support + family involvement + continue breastfeeding | SSRI + psychotherapy | Admission + antipsychotic + ECT |
| **Safety concern**| None | Monitor suicidal ideation | **Harm to baby — isolate baby immediately** |

> 🎯 **Clinical Clues**
> * Postpartum psychosis + Fever → Rule out **infection**
> * Postpartum psychosis + No fever → Do **urine toxicology**
> * Recurrence risk: psychosis 15–20%, depression 20–40%
> * Prophylactic antipsychotic during pregnancy = **NOT recommended** (give after delivery)

> ⏱ **Timing Pearl:** Highest timing to develop psychiatric disorder in pregnancy = **Puerperium (postpartum period)**
