# Vascular Surgery — AMC1 Notes

---

## Acute Limb Ischemia

- **Most common cause:** Embolism
- **Most common source of emboli:** Heart
- **Most common site:** Bifurcation of femoral artery
- **Earliest sign:** Pallor
- **Latest sign:** Paresis

⚡ H/o AF or MI + sudden severe leg pain → **acute embolization**

**Inv:** CT angiography (best)

**TTT:**
1. IV morphine (analgesia)
2. **IV heparin immediately** ← 1st step
3. Embolectomy ← 2nd step

---

## Acute Compartment Syndrome

- causes : RTA & Fractures (Truma)
- Marked limb swelling → vessel compression → ischemia
- **Characteristic finding:** Severe pain on **passive stretch** 
- Pressure in leg **>30 mmHg**
- **TTT: Immediate fasciotomy**

---

## Arterial Injuries

- **1st step:** Pressure to stop bleeding
- **Definitive TTT:** Surgery

---

## Chronic Limb Ischemia

- **Most common risk factor:** Atherosclerosis (smoking #1)
- **Most common artery:** Superficial femoral artery

**Symptoms (in order of severity):**
1. **Intermittent claudication** ← earliest
2. **Rest pain** ← most serious
   - Increases with leg elevation
   - Decreases by hanging legs down
   - Maximum at night
3. Ulceration and gangrene (severe)

**signs**

- Rubor on dependence
- (+) Burger test 

**Inv:**
- **1st investigation:** Duplex (ankle-brachial pressure index)
- **Investigation of choice:** CT angiography

**TTT:**
- Aspirin + statin

**Indications for surgery:**
- Rest pain, ischaemic ulceration, or gangrene
- Claudication limiting work or lifestyle

**Types of operation:**
| Extent | Procedure |
|---|---|
| Small segment | Thromboendarterectomy |
| Large segment | Bypass graft |
| Gangrene | Amputation |

- Most common complication after bypass graft → **restenosis**
- Severity depends mainly on **collateral circulation**

## Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm (AAA)

- **Most common risk factor:** Smoking
- **Usually asymptomatic** — expansile (pulsatile) abdominal mass
- **Risk:** Rupture → emergency

**Ruptured AAA — C/P:**
1. Severe abdominal pain referred to the back
2. Hypotension + tachycardia (hypovolaemic shock)

**Inv:** US (initial) — CT (best, if haemodynamically stable)

**TTT:** If ruptured → immediate resuscitation + urgent surgery

**Indications for elective surgery:**
- Size **>5.5 cm** OR Symptomatic (regardless of size)
- Increases **>1 cm/year**

## Buerger's Disease

- **Classic patient:** Young male smoker with amputated leg
- **Cause:** Neurovascular inflammation
- **C/P:** Recurrent claudication, superficial thrombophlebitis, Raynaud's phenomenon
- **TTT:** Stop smoking, sympathectomy, amputation in severe cases

---

## Carotid Endarterectomy

**Indications:**
1. **Asymptomatic** + ≥ 80% carotid stenosis
2. **Symptomatic** (TIA or stroke) + > 50% stenosis

**Contraindications:**
- Severe neurological deficit after cerebral infarction
- Completely occluded carotid artery
- Concurrent illness limiting life expectancy

---

## Carotid Dissection

- **C/P:** Sudden severe unilateral headache + neck pain + **Horner syndrome**
- **Inv:** CT with contrast
- **TTT:** Surgery

## DVT

- **C/P:** Pain, tenderness, swelling (most important sign)
- **Investigation:** Duplex

**Treatment:**
- Start **LMWH (enoxaparin) immediately** → then start warfarin
- Continue bridging LMWH until **INR >2** → then stop LMWH, continue warfarin only
- **Target INR: 2–3**

**Duration of warfarin:**
| Situation | Duration |
|---|---|
| Provoked DVT | 3–6 months |
| Unprovoked DVT | 6 months |
| Cancer | Until cancer treated |
| Pregnancy | Until 4–6 weeks postpartum |
| Antiphospholipid syndrome | 12 months |

⚡ Patient on heparin + warfarin, INR = 1.8 → **continue heparin + increase warfarin dose**

---

## DVT Prophylaxis

**Who needs it:** After major surgery — orthopaedic, GIT, high-risk pregnancy

**Duration:**
| Situation | Duration |
|---|---|
| Orthopaedic (hip/knee) | 35 days |
| Major surgery (>45 min, age >40) | Until discharge or 28 days |
| High-risk pregnancy | 4–6 weeks postpartum |
| Travel + high risk | Duration of flight |

---

## Bridge Therapy

**Who needs it:**
1. AF with high risk
2. Artificial valve
3. Recent thromboembolism

**How:**
- Stop warfarin 5 days before surgery → start LMWH as bridge
- Stop heparin **24 hours before** surgery → resume **24 hours after** surgery
- ⚡ Renal impairment → use **unfractionated heparin** (not LMWH)

---

## Drugs to Stop Before Surgery

| Drug | Stop how many days before |
|---|---|
| Warfarin | 5–7 days |
| Aspirin / Clopidogrel / NSAIDs | 5–7 days |
| Amitriptyline | 5–7 days |
| Metformin | 1 day |

## Anticoagulant Complications

### Supratherapeutic INR

**No bleeding:**
- INR >4.5 → reduce or stop next dose
- INR 4.5–10 → stop warfarin
- INR >10 → stop warfarin + give **vitamin K**

**With bleeding (clinically significant or life-threatening e.g. intracranial):**
1. Stop warfarin
2. Vitamin K
3. FFP **or** PCC ← most important

---

### Hematoma Formation

- Common sites: thigh, calf, **rectus sheath**
- drug that interacts with warfarin (eg. ABX & amiodarone)
- Rectus sheath hematoma → **FFP or PCC**

---

### Heparin-Induced Thrombocytopenia (HIT)

- **Cause:** Antibodies activating platelets → thrombosis (venous or arterial) + thrombocytopenia
- **TTT:** Stop heparin → may give direct thrombin inhibitor
- ⚡ **NEVER give platelets in HIT** → worsens condition

## Types of Lower Limb Ulcers

| Feature | Ischaemic | Venous | Neuropathic |
|---|---|---|---|
| Most common site | Tip of fingers/toes | **Medial malleolus** | Pressure sites (heel, 1st metatarsal head) |
| Painful? | **Yes** | No | **No** |
| Main TTT | Revascularisation | Dry/hydrocolloid dressing + compression stocking + leg elevation | Debridement + offloading |

## Diabetic Foot

- **Most common cause of ulceration:** Neuropathy + ischaemia
- **Most common sites:** Heel + head of 1st metatarsal (pressure areas)
- **Most effective prevention:** Foot care + good glycaemic control

**Treatment sequence:**
1. **Debridement** ← 1st and most important step
2. Antibiotics
> If clean ulcer with clear discharge (no infection signs) → dressing only, no antibiotics

**Best investigation:** Tissue/biopsy culture

**To exclude osteomyelitis (non-healing ulcer):**
- 1st → X-ray → if negative → **MRI**

**If gangrene → amputation**

**Severity classification:**
| Grade | Definition | TTT |
|---|---|---|
| Mild | Surrounding erythema <2 cm | Outpatient oral antibiotics |
| Moderate | Erythema >2 cm OR deeper structures involved (abscess, osteomyelitis, fasciitis) | Outpatient or inpatient IV antibiotics |
| Severe | Systemic signs (fever, tachycardia, tachypnoea, leukocytosis) | Inpatient IV antibiotics |

**Organisms to cover:**
| Organism | Antibiotic |s
|---|---|
| MRSA | Clindamycin or Vancomycin |
| Pseudomonas | Cefepime, piperacillin-tazobactam, meropenem |

⚡ Penicillin allergy → **cannot give** ticarcillin or piperacillin
