

## Shoulder & Upper Limb

**Anterior Shoulder Dislocation:**
- **Joint:** Glenohumeral
- **C/P:** Can't raise arm above head & loss of sensation over shoulder
- **Nerve:** Axillary nerve
- **TTT:** Closed reduction + sling
---

**Fracture Neck of HUMERUS** → Axillary nerve

**Fracture Shaft of HUMERUS** → Radial nerve

---

**Elbow Dislocation:**
- **Vessel:** Brachial artery → ischemia → immediate reduction if ischemic signs

**Supracondylar Fracture:**
- **Vessel:** Brachial artery → ischemia → immediate reduction
- **Long-term complication:** Volkmann contracture
---

**Fracture Radius & Ulna:**
- **Risk:** Compartment syndrome
- **TTT of compartment syndrome:** Immediate **fasciotomy**

---

**Colles' Fracture:** 3F = Female + FOOSH + Flexed position
- **Reduction:** Disimpaction → traction in flexed position with ulnar deviation
- **Cast:** Below elbow, pronated, semi-flexed, ulnar deviation
- **2nd look:** X-ray after 2 weeks
- Prolonged pain → analgesia + physical therapy

---

**Scaphoid Fracture:** Most common carpal bone + FOOSH
- **C/P:** Tenderness in anatomical snuff box
- **Inv:** X-ray changes appear only after 2 weeks → repeat X-ray  
- **TTT:** Thumb spica cast; if unstable → **ORIF**

---

**Clavicular Fracture:** FOOSH — most common site: middle third

| Type | Reduction | TTT |
|---|---|---|
| Middle third | None needed | Broad arm sling × 2 weeks |
| Lateral third | None if undisplaced; ortho referral if displaced | Broad arm sling |
| Medial third | None if undisplaced; urgent referral if displaced | Broad arm sling |

---

**Mallet Finger:** Trauma to extended finger
- **TTT:** Hyperextension of DIP joint × 6 weeks + non-adhesive tape to keep extended

---

**Tennis Elbow (Lateral Epicondylitis):**
- Inflammation of **extensor** muscle attachment at lateral elbow
- **C/P:** Pain at lateral elbow
- **TTT:** 1st → NSAIDs; 2nd → brace under elbow + **physical therapy** 

**Golfer's Elbow (Medial Epicondylitis):** Flexor muscles — medial side

## Hip & Lower Limb

**Posterior Hip Dislocation:**
- **Cause:** Car accidents 
- **C/P:** Short leg with **internal** rotation
- **Nerve:** Sciatic nerve 
- **TTT:** Closed reduction

---

**Femoral Fracture:**
- **C/P:** Short leg with **external** rotation
- **Risk:** Fat embolism
- **TTT:** Intramedullary nail fixation

**Tibial Fracture:** Direct trauma → risk: compartment syndrome

**Fracture Fibula:** Nerve → common peroneal

---

**Stress Fracture:**
- **Sites:** 2nd metatarsal (most common), proximal tibia
- **Risk:** Runners, **jumpers**, anorexia nervosa
- **C/P:** Pain with activity → relieved by rest; localised tenderness
- **X-ray:** Usually **normal**
- **TTT:** Rest + analgesia; cast helps healing 

---

**Osgood-Schlatter Disorder:** Adolescents + jumpers/runners
- **Cause:** Traction on immature tibial tubercle by patellar tendon
- **C/P:** Knee/patellar pain + **tenderness at tibial tubercle**
- **TTT:** Rest + NSAIDs + activity restriction — resolves in **12–18 months**

## Knee Injuries

| Feature | Meniscal Injury | Cruciate Injury |
|---|---|---|
| C/P | Locking (can't fully straighten) + give-way | "Pop" on twisting → swelling |
| Most common | Medial > lateral | ACL > PCL |
| Tests | McMurray (meniscal), varus/valgus stress (collateral) | ACL: anterior drawer, Lachman, pivot shift — PCL: posterior drawer |
| Inv | MRI | MRI |
| TTT | Conservative | MCL/PCL → conservative; **ACL/LCL → surgery** |

---

**Rupture Achilles Tendon:**
- running/jumping → sudden severe pain at ankle
- **Best test:** Calf squeeze → **no ankle movement**
- **TTT:** Surgery if <3 hours 
- ⚡ **Quinolones (ciprofloxacin) can rupture the Achilles tendon** HIGH YIELD

---

**Ankle Fracture/Dislocation:**
- Risk: vessel injury → check pulse
- No pulse → **1st: reduction** → if still no pulse → exploration
- ⚡ Fracture + no pulse + lacerated wound → **1st: reduction → 2nd: debridement** 

---

**Fracture Pelvis:**
- Most common complication: **bleeding** → tie and band pelvis
- ⚡ **NEVER advise rest** → DVT risk 
- Analgesia + **early mobilization** are essential

---

**Orbital Floor Fracture:**
- Vertical diplopia + enophthalmos
- ⚡ **Cheek numbness** → inferior orbital nerve injury *(key exam finding)*

**Zygomatic Fracture:**
- Vertical diplopia (upward gaze) + difficulty in mastication + enophthalmos

**Nasal Fracture:**
- **TTT:** Closed reduction; if deformity persists → rhinoplasty

---

## Post-op Management After Orthopedic Surgery

1. **Early mobilization** ← most important
2. LMWH prophylactic dose up to **6 weeks** (start **before** surgery, continue after)
3. **DEXA scan** on discharge (for underlying osteoporosis)

## Bone Pathology

**Paget's Disease:** Remodeling disorder — usually asymptomatic 
- Bony pain at night + skull deformity → deafness
- **Inv:** ↑ alkaline phosphatase — X-ray → lytic lesions
- **TTT:** Bisphosphonate

---

**Osteosarcoma:**
- **Age:** Up to 15 years 
- Painful movement
- **X-ray:** Subcortical new bone formation + periosteal elevation
- ⚡ Teenager + trauma + metaphysis swelling + periosteal elevation → **osteosarcoma** 

**Osteoclastoma (Giant Cell Tumour):** X-ray → soap bubble appearance

---

## Paediatric Orthopedics

**Metatarsus Adductus:**
- Adducted foot, bean-shaped sole
- Heel **CAN** go flat *(contrast with club foot)* 
- Resolution by age 3; **TTT: serial casting**; surgery if no resolution after 3 years

**Club Foot (Talipes Equinovarus):**
- Heel **CANNOT** go flat
- **TTT:** Corrective shoes + serial casting

**Internal Tibial Torsion:** Resolves by age **3–4** — observe only

**Medial Femoral Torsion:** Resolves by age **8–9** — reassure and observe

## Non-Orthopedic Topics

---

**Meralgia Paraesthetica:**
- **Key word:** Numbness/pain in **outer thigh**
- **Nerve:** Lateral cutaneous nerve of thigh
- **Causes:** OA, tight underwear/clothing/belt 
- **Inv:** X-ray hip 

---

**Trendelenburg Sign:**
- Weak abductor muscles (gluteus medius + minimus)
- **TTT:** Weight-bearing exercises over hip muscles 

---

**Dental Disorders:** Best imaging → orthopantomogram

**Ruptured Biceps Tendon:** Cannot abduct arm >40° with actively contracting deltoid

---

**Multiple Myeloma:** Elderly male + back pain + anaemia
- ⚡ Elderly male + chronic back pain + anaemia → **MM until proved otherwise**
- ⚡ Young male + chronic back pain → **ankylosing spondylitis** 

**C/P:** Bone pain, anaemia, weight loss, hypercalcaemia (constipation, polyuria, thirst), renal failure (Bence Jones proteins) , impaired immunity

**Inv:**
- ↑ Calcium, ↑ plasma cells (>10%), ↑ M proteins
- Blood film → **rouleaux cells** 
- X-ray → osteolytic lesions
- Back pain → MRI
- **Best:** Bone marrow biopsy

**TTT:** Chemotherapy (thalidomide)

⚡ Normal calcium does **NOT** rule out multiple myeloma 

---

**MGUS** 

Diagnosis (all 4 must be present):
1. Monoclonal paraprotein **<3 g/dL**
2. Plasma cells **<10%**
3. No bone lesions, anaemia, hypercalcaemia, or renal failure
4. No other B-cell disorder

| Feature | Multiple Myeloma | MGUS |
|---|---|---|
| Plasma cells | **>10%** | **<10%** |
| Management | Chemotherapy | Annual serum electrophoresis |

May transform into multiple myeloma → **annual follow-up by serum protein electrophoresis**

---

**Carpal Tunnel Syndrome:**
- **Nerve:** Median nerve
- **C/P:** Pain + numbness at thumb, index, middle finger — worse at **night**
- **Complication:** Permanent weakness + numbness 
- **Inv:** Nerve conduction + EMG; Phalen test
- **TTT:** 1st → analgesia + **splint** ; 2nd → cortisone injection; Last → surgery

---

**Plantar Fasciitis:** 
- **C/P:** Foot pain that increases at **start** of walking then subsides
- **X-ray:** Bony spur
- **TTT:** NSAIDs — heals within **12–18 months**

---

**Superior Vena Cava Syndrome:** 
- **Cause:** SVC obstruction — usually **lung cancer**
- **C/P:** Face and neck congestion — worsened by elevating arms **(Pemberton sign)**
- **Inv:** CT chest
- **TTT:** 1st step → corticosteroids + radiation

---

**Spinal Cord Compression:** 
- **Cause:** Cancer metastasis (e.g. breast)
- **C/P:** Back pain with h/o cancer + UMNL signs below level of compression
- **Inv:** **Immediate MRI** (emergency)
- **TTT:** 1st → **dexamethasone** (steroid); 2nd → irradiation
