# Surgery & Urology — AMC1 Notes

## 🫀 Liver & Pancreas

---

**Amebic Liver Abscess:** Travel + bloody diarrhea + RUQ + fever
- **Organism:** *Entamoeba histolytica*
- **Hx:** Travel to endemic area
- **Labs:** Stool → trophozoites
- **TTT:** Metronidazole — **NOT surgery**

---

**Hydatid Cyst:** Farmer + jaundice + Abd pain + fever
- **Organism:** *Echinococcus granulosus*
- **Hx:** Contact with dogs or sheep
- **Risk:** Anaphylaxis if ruptured
- **Inv:** CT → eggshell calcification, one large cyst + multiple daughter cysts; Serology if CT inconclusive
- **TTT:** Surgery under albendazole
- **Prevention:** Hand washing + dog deworming

---

**Liver Malignancy**
- **Primary:** ↑ AFP — CT → solitary lesion — TTT: surgery + chemo + embolization
- **Secondary:** More common — usually **from the colon** — CT → multiple spots
- **Hepatic Adenoma:** Rare — related to OCP → stop OCP — normal labs
- **Hemangioma:** Accidental finding → observation only

---

**Acute Pancreatitis:** Gallstones + Abd pain radiating to back + shock
- **Causes:** Gallstones (most common **acute**) — Alcohol (most common **chronic**)
- **Complications:**
  1. **Pseudocyst** (most common overall) → epigastric mass, resolves spontaneously
     - Treat if: >6 weeks **OR** >6 cm **OR** infected → endoscopic drainage to stomach
  2. **Acute renal failure** → most common **early** complication
  3. **ARDS** → bilateral interstitial infiltrates
- **Inv:** ↑↑↑ amylase & lipase — CT only if severe or complicated
- **TTT:** NPO + IV hydration + analgesia (conservative)
- ⚡ *Gallstone hx + abdominal pain + guarding + shock → pancreatitis*

---

**Pancreatic Cancer:** Elderly male + weight loss + jaundice
- **Risk factors:** Smoking (most important), DM, alcohol, **recurrent thrombophlebitis** ⬅️
- **Inv:** ↑ direct bilirubin — US → dilated GB + head mass — **CT = inv of choice**
- **TTT:** Whipple's operation
- **Palliative:** Stent (1st) → stent again if obstructed → palliative bypass (2nd line)
- ⚡ *Elderly + weight loss + jaundice → immediate CT*

---

**Chronic Pancreatitis:**
- **Causes:** Alcoholism, cystic fibrosis
- **Symptoms:** Epigastric pain, steatorrhea, DM, weight loss
- **Inv:** Amylase & lipase → **normal** — CT → **calcification**
- **TTT:** Pancreatic enzyme replacement

## 🦠 GIT

---

**Carcinoid Tumor:**
- **C/P:** Flushing, diarrhea, wheezing, cardiac murmur
- **Sites:** Appendix (most common), terminal ileum
- **Inv:** 24-hour urine **5-HIAA**
- **TTT:** <2 cm → appendicectomy; >2 cm → right hemicolectomy + 5-FU

---

**IBD Comparison:**

| Feature | Crohn's | Ulcerative Colitis |
|---|---|---|
| Site | Small + large bowel | Large bowel only |
| Lesions | Skip lesions | Continuous, no skip |
| Perianal | Fistula, anal fissure, ischiorectal abscess | Never |
| Oral ulcers | Yes | Never |
| Appearance | Cobblestone | — |
| Complications | Ca oxalate stones, cholesterol gallstones | Colon cancer (8–10 yrs), primary sclerosing cholangitis |
| Inv | **ASCA** + sigmoidoscopy/colonoscopy | **ANCA** + sigmoidoscopy |

**Extra-intestinal (both):** Episcleritis, iritis, ankylosing spondylitis, arthritis, erythema nodosum, pyoderma gangrenosum

**TTT:**
- Maintenance → sulfasalazine + mesalamine
- Acute flare → steroids
- Severe → azathioprine / cyclosporine

**Crohn's extra pearls:** ⬅️
- Most common cause of perianal fistula → **abscess**
- Multiple/recurrent fistulae or fissures → **Crohn's**
- Anal fissure with Crohn's → **Infliximab** (not standard fissure TTT)
- Internal abscess >5 cm → **CT-guided drainage**

**Vesicocolic Fistula** (Crohn's or diverticular disease): ⬅️
- Recurrent UTI with GI bugs + **pneumaturia** → classic stem

---

**Coeliac Disease:** Young + steatorrhea + iron/folate deficiency + fatigue
- **C/P:** FTT, fatigue, Fe + folate deficiency anaemia, steatorrhea, abdominal distension
- **Inv:**
  - Gold standard → duodenal biopsy → **atrophic villi**
  - Screening → anti-endomysial Ab, **anti-transglutaminase Ab**, anti-gliadin Ab ⬅️
  - Blood film → Howell-Jolly bodies
- **Associations:** Type 1 DM, IgA deficiency, autoimmune thyroiditis, dermatitis herpetiformis, osteoporosis ⬅️
- **Complications:** Intestinal lymphoma (most serious), dermatitis herpetiformis, osteoporosis
- **TTT:** Gluten-free diet + vitamin replacement + pneumococcal vaccine + Dapsone (for dermatitis)
- ⚡ *Steatorrhea + folate/iron deficiency → coeliac*
- ⚡ *Steatorrhea starting immediately after weaning → coeliac*

## 🧪 Urology

---

**Nephrolithiasis:**
- **C/P:** Sudden flank pain → groin, nausea/vomiting, hematuria
- **Inv:** CT KUB (best) — US (pregnant) — X-ray before surgery
- **Analgesic outside hospital:** NSAIDs

**Stone Types:**
| Type | Notes |
|---|---|
| Ca oxalate | Most common, radiopaque |
| Uric acid | Radiolucent |
| Struvite | Alkaline urine, Proteus |
| Cystine | Hexagonal, familial |

**TTT by size:**
- <0.5 cm → passes spontaneously
- <2 cm → ESWL
- >2 cm → percutaneous nephrolithotomy
- Staghorn → nephrolithotomy
- All patients → excess hydration + **thiazides** ⬅️

**Indications for surgery:** Obstruction + infection (most absolute), too large, uncontrolled pain

**Special cases:** ⬅️
- HIV on indinavir → radiolucent stone → investigate with **US**
- Young patient demanding strong analgesic → **1st step: drug screen** (take urine sample in front of you)

**Urosepsis:** ⬅️
- Most common cause of fever after urology procedure → bacteremia
- Empirical TTT → **amoxicillin + gentamycin**

---

**Renal Cell Carcinoma:**
- **C/P:** Flank pain + hematuria + renal mass + **↑ erythropoietin → polycythemia** ⬅️
- **Inv:** CT

**Management of incidental renal mass:** ⬅️
| Size | Action |
|---|---|
| <3 cm | Partial nephrectomy |
| ≥3 cm | Biopsy first |
| Biopsy malignant + <4 cm | Laparoscopic partial nephrectomy |
| Biopsy malignant + ≥4 cm | Total nephrectomy |
| With metastasis | Total nephrectomy + immunotherapy |

- Laparoscopic partial nephrectomy if: tumor <4 cm, bilateral cancer, or single working kidney
- Old age / poor candidate → **active surveillance** (CT for monitoring)
- Young age → active surveillance NOT recommended

---

**Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD):**
- **Genetics:** Autosomal dominant
- **C/P:** Painless hematuria, hypertension, bilateral flank mass
- **Associations:** Berry aneurysm → SAH, hepatic cysts, **mitral valve prolapse**, diverticulosis ⬅️
- **Inv:** US (1st), CT (best)
- **Fate:** Renal failure
- ⚡ *PKD + sudden severe headache → SAH*

---

**Rupture of Urethra:**
- Trauma + **blood at urethral meatus** (key word)
- **NEVER insert catheter**
- **Inv:** Retrograde urethrography

---

**Acute Prostatitis:**
- Fever, chills, urgency/dysuria, **tender prostate**
- **Inv:** Mid-stream urine culture
- **TTT:** Antibiotics

---

**BPH:**
- **C/P:** Frequency, nocturia **(1st symptom)**, hesitancy, weak stream
- **DRE:** Smooth + firm enlargement
- **TTT:** Alpha blockers (e.g., prazosin), TURP, surgery

**TURP Complications:**
| Complication | Timing |
|---|---|
| Bleeding | Most common short-term |
| Retrograde ejaculation | Most common long-term |
| TURP syndrome (hyponatremia) | Limit procedure to <1 hour |
| Bladder neck stenosis | Long-term |
| Urinary incontinence | Long-term |

---

**Prostatic Cancer:**
- Most common malignancy in men
- **Risk factors:** Age + family history
- **C/P:** Same as BPH
- **DRE:** Hard + asymmetric
- **Complication:** Osteoblastic metastases to vertebrae → back pain
- **Inv:** PSA → transrectal US + biopsy

**Treatment by stage:**
| Stage | TTT |
|---|---|
| Focal | Radical prostatectomy |
| Locally advanced | Radiation |
| Metastatic | Androgen ablation |
| Very old/unfit | Watchful waiting |

**Treatment by Gleason score:** ⬅️
| Gleason | TTT |
|---|---|
| ≤6 | Active surveillance OR radical prostatectomy |
| 7 | Radiation |
| 8–10 | Androgen ablation (orchidectomy or anti-androgen) |

**Treatment by PSA:** PSA <10 → active surveillance

**Active surveillance — who qualifies:** ⬅️
- ≤1 of 4 cores positive
- Gleason <6
- PSA <10 ng/mL

**Triggers to intervene:** Higher volume on biopsy OR PSA doubling time <3 years

**Screening:** ⬅️
- Not routinely recommended
- If patient asks → **1st: counsel and recommend against; 2nd: do it if patient insists**
- Most common side effect of prostatectomy → **erectile dysfunction**

**DD: Myeloma vs Prostate Cancer** (both = old male + back pain): ⬅️
| Feature | Multiple Myeloma | Prostate Cancer |
|---|---|---|
| Calcium | Hypercalcemia | Normal/high |
| Bone lesions | **Osteolytic** | **Osteoblastic** |

## 🫁 Scrotum & Testis

---

**Idiopathic Scrotal Oedema:** ⬅️
- Rapid, painless, severe scrotal edema extending to perineum/penis
- **Resolves spontaneously** — no intervention needed

---

**Varicocele:**
| Feature | Primary | Secondary |
|---|---|---|
| Side | Usually left | Either |
| Patient | Young | Old |
| Cause | Idiopathic | Malignancy |
| Onset | Gradual | Sudden |
| On elevation | Empties | Does NOT empty |
| Investigation | **US** ⬅️ | **CT abdomen/pelvis** ⬅️ |

- **C/P:** Scrotal discomfort + "bag of worms"
- **TTT:** Conservative; surgery if semen abnormality

---

**Epididymo-orchitis:**
- **Organism:** E. coli (most common); Chlamydia if <35 + sexually active
- **C/P:** Fever, pain, swelling, redness
- **Elevation of testis → decreases pain** (unlike torsion)
- **Sequence:** ⬅️ 1st → urine analysis + culture; 2nd → PCR for chlamydia/gonorrhea → then antibiotics

---

**Epididymal Cyst / Spermatocele:** ⬅️ *(entirely new)*
- **C/P:** Painless scrotal swelling — you **can get above it** — above and behind testis — **translucent**
- Translucent → hydrocele or epididymal cyst; Spermatocele → **radio-opaque**
- **Sequence:** Exam → US → tumor markers → review 3–6 months
- **TTT:** Small → no treatment; Large → **transscrotal surgery**

---

**Torsion of Testis:**
- **Age:** 5–15 years
- **Predisposing factor:** Transverse lying testis
- **C/P:** Sudden severe pain, swelling, nausea/vomiting
- **Elevation does NOT relieve pain** — absent cremasteric reflex
- **Inv:** Doppler (only if surgery not immediately available)
- **TTT:** URGENT SURGERY

---

**Testicular Cancer:**
- Young male + **painless** testicular enlargement
- **Spread:** Lung + para-aortic nodes
- **Types:** Seminoma (good prognosis), Non-seminoma (bad prognosis)

| Marker | Seminoma | Non-seminoma |
|---|---|---|
| AFP | Always **normal** | Increased |
| β-HCG | Increased | May increase |

- **Inv:** US (1st) → CT chest/abdomen; **NEVER needle biopsy**
- **TTT:** Orchidectomy always 1st — even with metastasis → then chemo
- **Approach:** **Trans-inguinal** (cancer) vs **transscrotal** (epididymal cyst)

---

**Undescended Testis:**
- **Complications:** Sterility, trauma, torsion, malignancy
- **Association:** Indirect inguinal hernia
- **TTT:** Surgery before **age 1**

## 🩸 Hematuria

- **Painless** → bladder cancer (most serious), kidney cancer
- **Painful** → stones, infection, kidney infarction

**Investigation pathway:**
1. Urine microscopy + culture
2. If normal → CT IVP + urine cytology (allergic/renal issue → US)
3. Cytology abnormal → **cystoscopy**

**Key points:**
- Immigrant + hematuria → schistosomiasis
- Hematuria + cyclophosphamide → **stop cyclophosphamide**
- Best inv for bladder → cystoscopy; for kidney → CT; for stones → CT KUB

## 🫁 Miscellaneous

---

**Lung Abscess:**
- **X-ray:** Air-fluid level
- **TTT:** Antibiotics (main) + physiotherapy → if no improvement → transpleural drainage

**Empyema:**
- **Most common cause:** Spread of lung infection
- **TTT:** Antibiotics + **chest tube**

**Epidermoid Cyst (Sebaceous Cyst):**
- Firm to soft regular lump
- **Complication:** Inflammation/infection
- **TTT:** Removal
