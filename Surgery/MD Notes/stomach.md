# Surgery - Stomach
## * Hematemsis: Vomiting blood
* **Causes:** Peptic ulcer -> Most common, Esophageal varices, Esophageal & gastric Cancer, reflux esophagitis
* **TTT:** 1st step resuscitation
  * Then inv & definitive TTT: upper endoscopy
## * Acquired pyloric stenosis: 
* stricture in pyloros
* **C/P:** vomiting (1h) after meals
* **Causes:** Fibrosed Peptic ulcer
* **Inv:** physical -> succussion splash, X-ray -> dilated stomach
* **TTT:** Surgery
## * Peptic ulcer: 
* duodenal / gastric & stress ulcers
* **duodenal ulcers:** 2-3h after meals Most common - decrease with food
* **gastric ulcers:** ½ - 1h after meals Worse with food
* **Risk factors:** Smoking - caffeine & NSAIDs imp
  * H.Pylori -> Most common cause
* **C/P:**
  1. Epigastric pain
  2. Nausea & vomiting & hematemsis
  3. Iron deficency anemia

# Inv for H. Pylori
## * Inv: 
1. look for H. Pylori -> urea breath test & Stool antigen
2. Serology IgG
3. Endoscopy & biopsy
## * TTT for H.pylori:
* **Triple Therapy 10-14 days:** 1) Amoxicillin 2) Clarithromycin 3) omeprazole
* **IF failed -> clarithromycin resistance:** 1) metronidazole 2) omeprazole 3) Bismuth 4) tetracyclin 
## * How to follow up after 10-14 days:
1. urea breath test -> Stop ABX 4 weeks & PPI 2 wk
* **IF Patient still Symptomatic:**
2. urea breath test -> Quadruple therapy
## * Complication of Peptic ulcer:
1. **Hematemesis ->** vomiting blood with shock symptoms
   * **MgMt:** GCS < 8 -> intubate
   * give packed RBCs Resuscitate
   * IV omeprazole
   * Endoscopy -> Inject adrenaline or heat probe
   * if not controlled medically -> Surgery
2. Perforated Peptic ulcer
* **C/P:** Severe Abd pain reffered to back & shock
* **Signs:** Abdomen rigidity & guarding
* **X-ray:** Air under diaphragm *
* **Resuscitate & Surgery**

## * Complication of gastric surgery *
1. **Dumping syndrome:** Fullness - pain - diarrhea ½ h after meals. 1-3 hour -> hypoglycemia.
   * **TTT:** Meals rich in protein & fat - less carbohydrates.
2. **Band slip:** The part below the band prolapses through the band.
   * **C/P:** GERD sever & Nausea & vomiting.
   * **Inv:** Barium meal.
   * **TTT:** Surgery.
3. **Anastomosis leakage:** Pain in lt shoulder & pleural effusion (keyword). 3-10 days post op
   * **C/P:** Fever, Abd pain, Nausea & vomiting.
   * **Inv:** CT scan immediately.
   * **TTT:** NPO - ABX & Surgery.
4. **Afferent loop obstruction:** IO (Intestinal Obstruction) & Marked vomiting & Abd pain.
   * **Inv:** CT scan immediate.
   * **TTT:** Surgery.

# Anus
1. **Piles / Hemorrhoids:** Painless (keyword) rectal bleeding with or without defecation.
   * **Risk factors:** Constipation & Pregnancy
   * **C/P:** Painless bleeding with or without defecation
   * **Complications:** Painful with infection or thrombosis
   * **Inv:** Colonscopy in elderly to exclude malignancy
   * **TTT:** 
     * 1st step -> diet & stool softeners
     * 2nd step -> band ligation if severe -> surgery
2. **Perianal hematoma:** Painful bleeding & swelling Anal
   * If thrombosed -> Incision & evacuation
   * **MgMt:** 
      *  1st 24h -> aspiration 
      * 1-5 days -> incision 
      * `> 5 days` -> resolves spontaneously 
3. **Pilonidal sinus:** young male with dark dense hair 
   * **C/P:** infected cyst - pain - drainage of pus or blood
   * **TTT:** Remove the cyst with the tract
4. **Anal Fistula:** pus draining canal connecting skin to anal canal
   * **Cause:** Abscess -> perianal fistula & Constipation
   * **C/P:** purulent persistent discharge / Recurrent -> chronic
   * **Inv:** Proctoscopy - Fistulography
   * **TTT:** Surgery
5. **Anal Fissure:** Painful tear in the anal lining
   * **Causes:** Constipation & Straining - If multiple -> chronic
   * **C/P:** Sever pain with defecation
   * **Inv:** Anoscopy - DRE is contraindicated
   * **TTT:** Increase Fiber - Fluids & stool softeners
     * 1st TTT: Glycerid Trinitate ointment (GTN)
     * 2nd: local incision & steroid cream
     * If Severe -> lateral Sphincterotomy

# Hernia
* **Femoral hernia:** Multiparous woman + Inguinal swelling
  * **Site:** Medial to femoral vessels & Lat. to pubic tubercle
  * **MgMt:** Most common to complicate -> immediate referral
* **Incisional hernia:** Swelling with Hx of surgery 
  * **Exam ->** Coughing & Standing
* **Epigastric hernia:** 
   * Adult obese -> surgery
   * kid -> reassure
* **Aquired diaphragmatic hernia:** Hx of injury or trauma
  * **C/P:** difficulty breathing & tachypnea
  * **Inv:** X-ray -> intestine in chest - Bowel sounds in chest

## * In pediatrics *
1. **inguinal hernia** -> Serious
   * IF obstructed or strangulation -> immediate surgery
   * IF strangulated -> Surgery ASAP
   * IF reducible Rule 6-2:
     * birth - 6 wks -> Surgery 2 days
     * 6 wks - 6 months -> 2 wks
     * `> 6 months` -> 2 months
   * * exclude congenital hypothyroidism in infants with Inguinal hernia
2. **umbilical hernia:**
   * `< 4 years` -> observe
   * `> 4 years` -> surgery

---

# How to suspect complicated hernia
1. No impulse on cough
2. irreducible
3. sysmptoms of IO: vomiting, constipation & distention
* **Inv of choice:** X-ray
* **TTT:** 
  * pediatrics -> herniotomy
  * Adult -> hernioplasty (laparoscopic with mesh)

## * Divarication of recti *
* **Physical Exam ->** Raising up without support
* **Pregnancy & Kids ->** No TTT
* **Mild ->** physiotherapy
* **severe ->** surgery

## * Esophagus *
1. **Corrosive injury:** Alkaline solution or washing powder
   * Endoscopy in 1st 24 hours - gastric lavage is contraindicated
2. **Achalasia:** Narrowing of LES
   * **C/P:** dysphagia to solid & liquids - regurgitation
   * **Inv:** Barium meal: Rat tail appearance
   * Manometric study: weak peristaltic wave
   * **TTT:** Heller's operation - dilators - Botulinum toxin
3. **Esophageal spasm:** Retro sternal pain increased by hot & cold drinks
   * **Inv:** Barium meal -> Corkscrew appearance
   * **TTT:** CCB -> for the spasm, nitroglycerine

# Eosinophilic esophagitis
4. **Esonophilic esophagitis:** inflammatory Allergy to esophagus
   * **C/P:** Dysphagia + Allergy association -> keyword (Asthma or celiac disease)
   * **Inv:** Endoscope -> Rings, Ridges & furrows
   * **TTT:** 1st dietary modification 2nd swallowed steroid
5. **Esophageal stricture:** 
   * Hx of GERD -> GERD disappears -> dysphagia
   * **Inv:** Endoscopy
   * **TTT:** PPI & dilators
6. **Barret's esophagus:** long standing GERD -> Adenocarcinoma
   * **Inv:** endoscopy & biopsy
   * **TTT:** 
     * No dysplasia -> repeat endoscopy 3-5 years
     * low dysplasia -> repeat endoscopy 6 months
     * High dysplasia -> ablation or surgery
7. **Mallory Weiss $:** Alcoholic -> recurrent vomiting -> hematemesis
   * Gastro-esophageal lacerations
   * **C/P:** vomiting & hematemesis
   * **Inv:** Endoscopy
   * **TTT:** Cauterization or injection of epinephrine to stop bleeding

# Colon Cancer
1. **Familial polyposis coli:** AD - colon & rectal polyps
   * **Fate:** colon cancer
   * **Timing:** 10-15 yrs old screening: Colonoscopy
   * **TTT:** surgery once polyp appears
2. **Gardner $:** variant of FPC
   * Desmoid tumor & osteoma + epidermoid cyst
3. **Peutz-Jeghers $:** Antrolip pigmentation
   * **Future risk:** colon cancer & intussusception
4. **Non FPC (lynch $):** AD - colorectal cancer, endometrial cancer, GIT cancer & ovarian cancer
5. **Cancer colon:**

| Feature | Right colon (cecum) | Lt colon | Sigmoid | Rectum |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Symptom 1** | Iron deficiency anemia | Abnormal bowel habit | complete bowel obstruction | Bleeding |
| **Symptom 2** | Dyspepsia | partial obstruction | Altered bowel habits | Abdominal mass |
| **Symptom 3** | Abd mass | pain | bleeding | Tenesmus |

* **Inv:** 
  * X-ray: Apple core appearance
  * CT colonography: Intial diagnostic test
  * colonscopy: Investigation of choice
  * FOBT -> +ve
* **Screening:** colonscopy Every year, CEA in between

## * Cancer colon with local metastasis *
* **Preoprative:** chemo & Radio
* **Post operative:** chemo only

# IBS
* stress triggerred young people
* **C/P:** spasmodic Abd pain Alternating between diarrhea & constipation
* **TTT:** High fiber diet & laxatives
  * spasmolytics
  * SSRI -> DOC -> 2-4 weeks to work
## * Pseudomembranous Colitis: 
* diarrhea with Hx of ABX
* **C/P:** diarrhea
* **Inv:** stool analysis for PCR & antigen and toxin
* **TTT:** 1st line -> vancomycin
  * if severe -> IV metronidazole + vancomycin
## * Virchow's node: 
* LN in supra clavicular fossa
* takes it supply from lymph vessels in stomach
* **Significant for ->** Stomach cancer
* **Inv:** 1st -> CT neck, 2nd -> FNAC
