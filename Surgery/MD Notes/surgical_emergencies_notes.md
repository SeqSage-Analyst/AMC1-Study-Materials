# Surgical Emergencies — AMC1 Notes

---

## Airway

- **Establishing and securing the airway is always the 1st step**
- **Intubate if GCS ≤8**
- **Most common indication for intubation in trauma:** Altered mental status

**Intubate when:**
- GCS ≤8
- Burns to face/neck
- Hoarseness from allergy or burn
- Soot in the airway
- Severe asthma not controlled by medication

- ⚡ Failed intubation → **cricothyroidotomy**
- ⚡ Tracheostomy → done in **OR only**, not ER

**Cervical neck injury:**
- 1st step → **cervical collar**
- Airway → endotracheal intubation

---

## Haemodynamics & Bleeding

- ⚡ **Pulse change is the FIRST indicator of hypovolaemia** — BP drops only after ≥30% blood loss
- Drop in BP → absolute indication for blood transfusion
- **1st step:** 2 wide-bore IV lines
- **2nd step:** Fluid
- Blood needed → **packed RBCs (O− low haemolysin)**

---

## Chest Emergencies

### Pericardial Tamponade
- **C/P:** Hypotension + distant heart sounds + distended neck veins *(Beck's triad)*
- **ECG:** Electrical alternans
- **Vitals:** Pulsus paradoxus
- **X-ray:** Enlarged cardiac shadow (flask shape)
- **Inv of choice:** Echo
- **TTT:** Immediate **pericardiocentesis**
- ⚡ Sudden chest pain during pericardiocentesis → **pneumothorax** → immediate CXR
- ⚡ Sudden chest pain during pacemaker insertion → **pneumothorax** → immediate CXR

---

### Tension Pneumothorax
- **C/P:** Respiratory distress + tracheal deviation + distended neck veins + absent breath sounds
- **Percussion:** Hyperresonance
- **TTT:** Immediate large-bore needle into **2nd intercostal space, MCL** (needle decompression)

---

### Traumatic Aortic Rupture
- **Cause:** Severe chest wall trauma
- **Vitals:** Significant hypotension + tachycardia
- **X-ray:** **Widening of mediastinum** *(key word)*
- **TTT:** Surgery

---

### Rib Fracture
- **C/P:** Chest pain + dyspnoea
- **1st step:** Analgesia (e.g. morphine)
- ⚡ Old age + rib fracture + avoid opioids → **local nerve block**

---

### Flail Chest
- Multiple contiguous rib fractures → **paradoxical thoracic wall movements** *(key word)*
- Patient takes shallow breaths due to severe pain
- **TTT sequence:**
  1. Pain control + supplemental oxygen (morphine = choice)
  2. Mechanical ventilation
  3. Chest tube if pneumothorax present
- ⚡ Strapping the chest → **no longer done**

---

## Head Trauma

- **1st step always:** Head + neck CT **without** contrast
- Closed fracture + no symptoms → **no treatment**
- Comminuted or depressed skull → **surgery**

### Basal Skull Fracture
- **C/P:** Raccoon eyes + Battle's sign + CSF leak from ear or nose
- **Inv:** CT head and neck
- **Fate:** Most heal spontaneously
- ⚡ No antibiotics — No packing — No sniffing
- ⚡ Pupil constriction after head trauma → **urgent CT**

---

### Epidural Haematoma
- **Cause:** Trauma to side of head → rupture of **middle meningeal artery**
- **C/P:**
  1. Head trauma → sudden LOC
  2. **Lucid interval** (honeymoon period — patient wakes and appears normal)
  3. Ipsilateral pupil dilatation (oculomotor nerve compression)
- **Cushing's reflex** (↑ICP) → hypertension + bradycardia + respiratory depression
- **Complication:** Tentorial herniation — first nerve affected = **oculomotor** → mydriasis + ptosis
- **CT:** **Biconvex (lens-shaped)** haematoma
- **TTT:** Emergency craniotomy

---

### Subdural Haematoma
- **Bleeding:** Venous — bridging veins
- **Cause:** Minimal trauma — falling in elderly or alcoholic
- **C/P:** Gradual headache, memory loss, personality changes, confusion, drowsiness
- **High risk:** Alcoholics (repeated falls), elderly (brain atrophy)
- ⚡ Elderly + abnormal behaviour + personality change + fall → **subdural haematoma**
- **CT:** **Concave crescent** shape
- **TTT:** Conservative with steroids
- Emergency craniotomy if: **lateralising signs + midline displacement**

---

### Elevated ICP
- **Dx:** CT → midline shift or dilated ventricles
- ⚡ Never do lumbar puncture before CT → risk of **brain herniation**
- **TTT:** Head elevation 45° + hyperventilation + mannitol + furosemide + sedation + hypothermia

### Burr Hole (Trephine)
**All 3 needed:**
1. GCS <8
2. Imaging evidence of extradural haematoma with midline shift
3. Neurosurgical intervention not available in time

- Low GCS + tertiary hospital >2 hours away → CT then **burr hole**
- Low GCS + tertiary hospital <2 hours away → CT then **refer**

---

### Concussion
- **CT:** Normal
- **C/P:** Headache, dizziness, memory/concentration problems
- **TTT:** None — resolves spontaneously

---

## Abdominal Trauma

### Penetrating Trauma
- **1st step:** Direct pressure to control bleeding
- ⚡ Blind clamping / tourniquet → **never**
- ⚡ Knife embedded in patient → **never remove in ER** — only in OR under GA
- Next priority → IV lines + fluid resuscitation

**Knife at chest — management by O₂ saturation:**
| Situation | 1st Step |
|---|---|
| Low O₂ + tension pneumothorax | Needle thoracotomy |
| Low O₂ + haemothorax | Intubation |
| Low O₂ + low BP | Intubation |
| Normal O₂ + haemothorax | Chest tube |
| Normal O₂ + low BP | IV fluid resuscitation + cross-match |
| Low BP + blood ready | Give blood immediately |

- Remove knife only after: intubation → chest tube → GA in OR
- ⚡ Poor IV access in children or adults → **intraosseous cannulation** (proximal tibia)

---

### Blunt Abdominal Trauma
- **Most affected organs:** Spleen > liver > kidney

**Investigation:**
| Patient Status | Inv | Value |
|---|---|---|
| **Unstable** | **FAST** | Detects haemoperitoneum, pericardial effusion |
| FAST limited/equivocal | DPL | Evaluate haemoperitoneum |
| **Stable** | **CT with contrast** | Best for liver, spleen, kidney injury |
| Positive FAST or DPL | → Exploratory laparotomy | — |

---

### Spleen Injury (Most Common Injured Organ)
- **C/P:** LUQ pain + left lower chest wall tenderness + **left shoulder pain** (phrenic/diaphragm irritation)
- Delayed hypotension → dislodged thrombus
- **Dx:** CT abdomen with IV contrast

**Management by stability:**
| Situation | TTT |
|---|---|
| Vitally unstable | Immediate laparotomy |
| Stable + extravasation | Embolisation |
| Stable + no extravasation | Conservative |

---

### Liver Injury
- Most cases → **conservative treatment**
- Unstable → laparotomy

### Kidney Injury
- **C/P:** Haematuria + flank pain
- **Inv:** CT with contrast (best); FAST if unstable
- **TTT:** Most heal spontaneously

---

### Blunt Trauma — Specific Organs
- Blunt epigastric trauma → rupture of **stomach**
- Blunt chest trauma → rupture of **aorta or oesophagus**
  - Low BP → aortic rupture
  - Small pleural effusion → **oesophageal rupture**

---

## Acute Abdomen

### GI Perforation
- **C/P:** Sudden, severe, constant, generalised abdominal pain
- **Most common causes:** Diverticulitis, perforated peptic ulcer, Crohn's
- **Dx:** Erect abdominal X-ray → **free air under diaphragm**
- ⚡ Free air under diaphragm → **most absolute indication for immediate surgery**
- **TTT:** NPO + IV fluids + IV antibiotics + emergency surgery

---

### Oesophageal Perforation
- **Most common cause:** Iatrogenic (post-endoscopy)
- **C/P:** Chest/upper abdominal pain + dysphagia/odynophagia + **SC emphysema**
- **CXR:** Small pleural effusion
- **Dx:** **Gastrografin contrast oesophagogram** (investigation of choice)
- Surgical emergency

---

## Intestinal Obstruction

**Cause by history:**
| History | Most Likely Cause |
|---|---|
| Prior surgery (<5 years) | **Adhesions** (most common overall) |
| H/o hernia | Incarcerated hernia |
| Sudden onset in elderly | **Volvulus** |
| Elderly + anaemia + weight loss + melena | Cancer |
| Anti-Parkinsonian drugs | Pseudo-obstruction |

- Most common hernia to complicate → **femoral**

**C/P:** Colicky pain + vomiting + constipation + distension + hyperactive bowel sounds

**Dx:** Erect AXR → dilated loops with air-fluid levels; **CT = imaging of choice**

**TTT:** NPO + nasogastric decompression → if no improvement → surgery

⚡ Strangulation signs = fever + tachycardia + leukocytosis
⚡ Severe pain referred to back → **perforation**

**Small vs Large Bowel Obstruction:**
| Feature | Small Bowel | Large Bowel |
|---|---|---|
| Location on X-ray | Central, no haustration | Peripheral, haustration |
| Most common cause | Adhesions | **Colon cancer** |

⚡ Old male + constipation + prior appendicectomy + IO signs → **colon cancer until proved otherwise**
⚡ Bilious vomiting in infants = IO → 1st step conservative TTT

---

### Fecal Impaction vs Volvulus

| Feature | Fecal Impaction | Volvulus |
|---|---|---|
| Key words | Bedridden / nursing home | Sudden onset IO symptoms |
| C/P | Chronic constipation + **fecal soiling** (watery leakage) | Same as IO + sudden onset |
| DRE | **Full** rectum | **Empty** rectum |
| X-ray | — | **Omega loop** |
| Main TTT | Enema + disimpaction | Decompression (sigmoid) / Surgery (caecal) |

- Sigmoid volvulus → **1st line: flexible sigmoidoscopy decompression**
- Caecal volvulus → **1st line: surgery** (colonoscopy NOT effective)

---

### Intussusception
- **Most common type:** Ileocaecal
- **More common in:** Children
- **Predisposing factor:** URTI
- **C/P:** Colicky pain + screaming + legs drawn to abdomen + **red currant jelly stool**
- **Exam:** Sausage-shaped mass
- **Inv of choice:** Ultrasound
- **TTT:** **Air enema reduction** (diagnostic + therapeutic)

---

### Paralytic Ileus
- **Causes:** Abdominal surgery, spine fracture, hypokalaemia, uraemia, DKA, peritonitis
- **C/P:** Distension + vomiting + constipation — ⚡ **NO pain** — silent abdomen
- **TTT:** Correct cause + conservative; surgery rarely needed

---

### Intestinal Pseudo-obstruction
- ⚡ Key word: **patient on anti-Parkinsonian drugs**
- C/P: Abdominal pain + severe distension + nausea + diarrhoea/constipation

---

### Acute Diverticulitis
- **Patient:** Old age male + constipation
- **C/P:** LLQ pain + fever + leukocytosis + tender LLQ mass
- **Imaging of choice:** CT
- ⚡ Never order contrast studies or endoscopy in acute phase
- **TTT:** Antibiotics + fluids
- Perforation/generalised peritonitis → emergency surgery
- Abscess >5 cm → surgical drainage

---

### Acute Appendicitis
- **Sequence:** Anorexia → periumbilical pain → sharp constant **RLQ pain** + fever + leukocytosis
- ⚡ **Most important sign: tenderness at right iliac fossa**
- **Inv of choice:** CT
- ⚡ Highly suggestive clinical picture → proceed to surgery without investigation
- **TTT:** Laparoscopic appendicectomy

**Complications of appendiceal perforation:**
1. **Psoas abscess** — psoas sign (pain on hip flexion against resistance) — TTT: IV fluids + antibiotics + surgery
2. **Pelvic abscess** (most common complication) — tender mass on PR + painful defecation + diarrhoea → abscess drainage

**Appendicitis in pregnancy:**
- Appendix displaced **upward toward liver**
- **Inv:** US (not CT — avoid radiation)
- **TTT:** Laparoscopic appendicectomy

---

### Toxic Megacolon
- **Emergent complication of ulcerative colitis**
- **C/P:** Severe abdominal pain + distension + fever
- **X-ray:** Massively distended transverse colon
- **TTT sequence:**
  1. NPO + IV fluids + antibiotics + **IV steroids**
  2. Nasogastric decompression
  3. If failed → surgery

---

### Acute Mesenteric Ischaemia
- ⚡ Key word: **h/o AF** → embolisation
- **C/P:** Sudden severe abdominal pain + bloody diarrhoea
- **Serum lactate:** Elevated
- **Inv of choice:** CT angiography
- **TTT:** Heparin (1st) → embolectomy

---

## Surgical Jaundice

| Cause | Key Clue | Inv of Choice |
|---|---|---|
| Pancreatic cancer | Old male + weight loss + jaundice | CT |
| Choledocholithiasis | Obese female 40s + RUQ pain + dilated bile duct | Abdominal US |
| Biliary colic | RUQ colicky pain → shoulder + back, fatty food trigger | US |
| Acute cholecystitis | RUQ pain + fever, cystic duct stone | US (thick-walled GB + pericholecystic fluid) |
| Acute cholangitis | Pain + fever + jaundice + confusion | ERCP (emergency) |

**Distinguishing by symptoms:**
- Pain only → biliary colic
- Pain + fever → cholecystitis
- Pain + jaundice → choledocholithiasis
- Pain + jaundice + fever + chills + confusion → **cholangitis**

---

### Acute Ascending Cholangitis
- **Cause:** CBD obstruction + ascending infection
- **Most common organism:** E. coli
- **C/P:** Pain + fever + chills + **jaundice + confusion** (Charcot's triad + confusion)
- **Inv:** ↑ alkaline phosphatase + ↑ direct bilirubin + ↑↑ WBC
- **TTT:**
  1. IV fluids ← 1st step
  2. Emergent ERCP (CBD decompression) ← lifesaving
  3. Cholecystectomy before discharge

---

### Gallbladder — Quick Rules

- **Most common gall stone type:** Cholesterol
- **Stone with haemolysis:** Pigmented (black)
- **Normal bile duct diameter:** ≤6 mm — if >6 mm → ERCP
- **No symptoms = no treatment** (even in pregnancy)
- **Once symptomatic = surgery**

**Cholecystitis + recent MI:**
- 1st step → conservative; delay surgery **6 months after MI**
- Severe symptoms → **percutaneous cholecystostomy**

**Gallstones in pregnancy:**
- No symptoms → no treatment
- Best time to remove → **2nd trimester**
- Must remove because → **increased risk of cholestasis**

**Indications for ERCP before cholecystectomy:**
- Dilated CBD (>6 mm)
- Obstructed CBD
- Cholangitis (after resuscitation)
- Post-cholecystectomy syndrome

---

### Gallstone Ileus
- Gallstone impacted in small bowel lumen
- **X-ray:** Pneumobilia + small bowel obstruction + radiopaque stone
- **TTT:** Fluid resuscitation + NGT suctioning → if failed → **enterolithotomy** (surgical emergency)

---

### Post-cholecystectomy Syndrome
- Persistence of symptoms after cholecystectomy
- **Causes:** Sphincter of Oddi dysfunction, biliary microlithiasis
- **1st inv:** US — **Inv of choice:** ERCP

---

## Urological Emergencies

**Priapism:**
- Painful persistent erection
- **TTT:** 1st → repeated saline flushing; 2nd → aspiration; Last → surgery

**Fracture Penis:**
- Cause: Trauma during intercourse
- **C/P:** Popping sound + severe pain
- **TTT:** Immediate surgery
