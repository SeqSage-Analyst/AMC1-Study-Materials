# Breast

## Main work up flow:
1. **1st** History & examination
2. **2nd** Imaging 
   * `< 40` -> U/S
   * `> 40` -> Mammography
3. **3rd** Biopsy 
   * Solid -> core biopsy
   * Cystic -> FNAC - aspirate

## * Fibroadenoma:
* Benign mass in young women (20s or 30s)
* **Mass feature:** Firm, mobile, smooth & non painful
* **Inv:** U/S -> hypoechoic mass
* **TTT:** remove if > 3 cm or patient above 40

## * Fibroadenosis:
* Cyclic mass according to menses
* **C/P:** pain, redness & swelling. Fluctuation in size & cyclic changes
* **Inv:** Mammography & FNAC
* **TTT:** symptomatic -> primrose oil & NSAIDs
* Supportive -> (tight bra) very imp.
* 2nd -> OCP
* 3rd -> Danzol

## * Intraductal papilloma:
* **bloody discharge**
* **Inv:** ductograph
* **TTT:** duct excision

---

## * Mammary duct ectasia:
* yellow serous creamy white discharge
* **cause:** dilatation of ducts
* **TTT:** Excision

## * Mastitis:
* staph aureus - lactating - cracked nipple
* **C/P:** fever, pain, redness & swelling
* **TTT:** Support breast - heat bags & ABx Flucloxacillin
* in lactating woman -> affected breast first if can't feed -> evacuate by a pump & give milk to baby

## * Acute breast abscess:
* fluctuation with fever, redness & pain
* **C/P:** Severe throbbing pain & high fever
* **TTT:** Incision & leave a drain
* **ABX:** Flucloxacillin

## * Gynecomastia:
1. **Infantile** -> Normal resolves in 6 months don't squeeze
2. **Pubertal** -> common 50% of teenagers, pain & swelling, reassure
3. **Senile** -> cause reduction in testicular function
   * consider medication induced (Digoxin, spironolactone)

# * Breast Cancer *

## Risk factors:
* old age - obesity - positive F.x - +ve BRCA1 & BRCA2
* Early menarche - late Menopause. late first pregnancy - Nullipara
* **Most common site:** upper outer quadrant

## Types:
1. **Ductal carcinoma** (Most common)
2. **Lobar carcinoma** (bilateral)
3. **Inflammatory carcinoma** (rare & very invasive)

## C/P:
* painless breast mass
* Skin: dimpling & ulceration
* Peau d orange: non-pitting lymph edema
* nipple: retracted
* enlarged lymph nodes

## Inv: 
* Mammography & Biopsy -> Microcalcification & Spiculated appearance

## * Paget's disease:
* Cancer without mass
* **C/P:** unilateral nipple erosion
* **TTT:** wide local excision of the nipple & underlying area

## * MgMt of Breast Cancer:
1. **Small & one foci** -> conservative surgery (wide local excision) & reconstructive surgery followed by -> radiation
2. **Large & Multiple** -> Mastectomy followed by -> radiation
3. **Sentinal LN biopsy**
   * `+ve` -> Axillary LN dissection & radiotherapy
   * `-ve` -> No further MgMt
4. **HER2 +ve** -> Trastuzumab
5. **ER +ve** * `post meno` -> Raloxifene -> osteoporosis
   * `pre meno` -> Tamoxifene -> DVT & endom. cancer
6. **BRCA 1/2 +ve** -> Mastectomy with prophylactic oophorectomy

---

## * Screening of breast cancer
* **Mammography** -> 50-74 -> every 2 years
* **when to screen younger age?**
  * 1st degree relative < 50
  * **OR**
  * 2 second degree relative on same side of family & at least one < 50

## When to do genetic testing?
TWO First degree or second degree on same side with breast cancer or ovarian cancer
**+ (Plus one of the following):**
1. bilateral breast cancer
2. breast cancer < 40
3. additional relative with breast or ovarian cancer
4. Male with breast cancer
5. breast & ovarian cancer in same woman
* Two First or 2nd degree on same side with breast < 45 & Sarcoma < 45

**If any patient has the above criteria do:**
1. genetic counselling
2. Preventative SERM
3. Mammography from age 40
4. MRI at age of 25

## * Breast hematoma
* Hx of trauma
* **C/P:** hard & painful mass
* **Inv:** breast biopsy -> fat globules
* **TTT:** Excision
