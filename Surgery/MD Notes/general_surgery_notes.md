# General Surgery — AMC1 Notes
### (Salivary Glands · Soft Tissue · Thyroid · Post-op · Burns · Miscellaneous)

---

## Salivary Gland Disorders

---

**Acute Parotitis:**
- **Cause:** Bad oral hygiene + dehydration
- **Organism:** Staph
- **C/P:** Painful swelling + pus from duct
- **Prevention:** Hydration + oral hygiene
- **TTT:** Antibiotics

---

**Salivary Gland Stones:**
- **Most common gland:** Submandibular
- **C/P:** Swelling and pain **increased by eating** — pain referred to the ear — enlarged tender gland
- **Inv:**
  - 1st → Intraoral X-ray
  - 2nd → Sialogram (if X-ray inconclusive)

---

**Salivary Gland Tumours:**
| Feature | Benign | Malignant |
|---|---|---|
| Growth | Slow, painless, well-defined | Rapid, painful |
| Facial nerve | **Not affected** | **Affected** |

- Most common **benign** → **pleomorphic adenoma**
- Most common **malignant** → mucoepidermoid carcinoma
- Bilateral or hot spots → **Warthin tumour**

⚡ Swelling in front of the ear + **facial nerve paralysis** → **malignant lesion**

**Investigation sequence:**

| Presentation | 1st Inv | Inv of Choice |
|---|---|---|
| Solid mass | CT | Biopsy (FNAB → if inconclusive → core biopsy → if cancer → CT head & neck) |
| Inflammation | Ultrasound | — |
| Stones | X-ray | Sialogram |

**TTT:**
- Benign → Superficial parotidectomy
- Malignant → Parotid removal
- ⚡ Main nerve at risk during surgery → **facial nerve**

## Soft Tissue Disorders

---

**Keloid vs Hypertrophic Scar:**
- **Keloid** → extends **beyond** the scar → cortisone can be used
- **Hypertrophic scar** → limited **to** the scar

---

**Acute Paronychia:** Infection of the nail bed
- **TTT:** Soaking + antibiotics + incision if abscess

**Hand Space Infection:** Direct inoculation
- **TTT:** Incision and drainage

---

**De Quervain's Tenosynovitis:**
- **Risk:** Repetitive wrist/thumb movements — new mothers, workplace activity
- **C/P:** Pain + swelling over radial styloid, worsened by thumb motion and wrist deviation
- **TTT:** 1st → NSAIDs + splint; 2nd → cortisone injection

---

**Dupuytren's Contracture:** Middle-aged men + family history
- **Most important cause:** Alcoholism; also trauma, smoking
- ⚡ Always check **blood glucose** (associated with DM)
- **Pathology:** Thickening + fibrosis + shortening of **palmar fascia**
- **C/P:** Flexed fingers — nodules at palmar fascia
- **Inv:** US (initial, must be done before surgery)
- **Recurrence rate:** High
- **TTT:** Early → physiotherapy; Definitive → **surgery**

---

**Volkmann's Ischaemic Contracture:**
- **Cause:** Most commonly supracondylar fracture → brachial artery injury → ischaemia → muscle fibrosis → permanent shortening
- **TTT:** Physiotherapy + surgery

---

**Ingrowing Toenail:**
- **Typical patient:** Young male + tight shoes + faulty nail trimming
- **TTT:** Gauze soaked in antiseptic + proper footwear; Definitive → **wedge resection**

## Thyroid & Neck

---

**Thyroglossal Cyst:**
- **Congenital** — persisting part of thyroglossal duct
- **Appears:** Late childhood
- **C/P:** Midline neck swelling that **moves up with tongue protrusion**
- **Most common fate:** Infection → therefore must be removed
- **TTT:** Surgical removal

---

**Thyroid Cancers:**
- More common in **females**

| Type | Key Feature |
|---|---|
| **Papillary** | Most common — psammoma bodies |
| **Follicular** | Diagnosed by capsular infiltration (NOT on FNAB alone) |
| **Medullary** | Familial |

- ⚡ Follicular cells on FNAB → next step → **core biopsy** (to assess capsular infiltration)
- ⚡ Enlarged cervical LN + biopsy shows thyroid tissue → **thyroid cancer with metastasis**
- ⚡ **Hoarseness of voice** → infiltration of RLN → **bad sign**
- **Lateral aberrant thyroid** → enlarged deep cervical LN with normal thyroid
- **Main treatment:** Total thyroidectomy

---

**Complications of Thyroidectomy:**

**1. Stridor (haematoma under fascia)**
- **TTT: Immediately remove all stitches and open wound at the ward**
- Applies to any neck surgery (e.g. carotid surgery)

**2. Thyroid Crisis (acute hyperthyroidism)**
- **Timing:** Immediately after surgery
- **C/P:** Hyperpyrexia, tachycardia, tachypnoea, hypertension, agitation, tremors, convulsions
- **Prevention:** Good pre-op preparation
- **TTT:** Propranolol + carbimazole + cortisone

**3. Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve (RLN) Injury**
- Presentation may be delayed days to weeks
- Unilateral → hoarseness
- Bilateral complete → aphonia + dyspnoea
- Bilateral incomplete → **aphonia + stridor**

**4. Hypoparathyroidism**
- **Cause:** Removal of all 4 parathyroid glands
- **C/P:** Perioral numbness + tetany
- **1st step:** IV calcium gluconate 10% slowly
- **Maintenance:** Calcium + vitamin D

## Post-operative Fever / Agitation

| Timing | Cause |
|---|---|
| Day 1–2 | **Atelectasis** |
| Day 2–3 | Endometritis / UTI / pneumonia |
| Day 5–7 | **PE / DVT** |
| Day 5–7 | Wound infection / abscess |

### 1. Atelectasis (Day 1)
- **C/P:** Fever + agitation + dyspnoea
- **Risk:** Old age, COPD, smokers (anaesthesia inhibits cough)
- **Sequence:** 1st → pulse oximetry; 2nd → ABG → then X-ray
- **TTT:** Oxygen first → breathing exercises (treatment of choice)
- **Best prevention:** Incentive spirometry

### 2. UTI — urgency, frequency, dysuria

### 3. Endometritis — high fever + foul lochia + uterine tenderness

### 4. Pneumonia — fever + cough + dyspnoea + secretions

### 5. Pulmonary Embolism (Day 5–7)
- Chest pain + dyspnoea + haemoptysis
- **Best inv:** CTPA

### 6. Wound Infection
- Pain + redness + swelling + discharge
- Fluctuant swelling → abscess → **incision and drainage**
- **TTT:** Antibiotics

### 7. Mastitis
- Breast pain + redness
- **Continue breastfeeding** — start from affected side
- If cannot breastfeed → evacuate with pump

## Post-operative Fluid Management

- **Daily requirements:** ~3 litres (2L dextrose 5% + 1L normal saline) + deficit
- **Deficit:** Given as normal saline
- **Potassium:** 20 mmol/L → minimum **60 mmol/day**
- **Urine output target:** >0.5–1.0 ml/kg/hour
- ⚡ Input ≠ output most commonly → **error in calculation**
- ⚡ Output > input on day 5 → **resolution of paralytic ileus**

**Post-operative Oliguria — sequence:**
1. Fluid challenge (250 cc bolus) → if no response
2. Urinary catheter or bladder US
3. Urea, creatinine, electrolytes
- Fever + oliguria → culture → ?sepsis

## Shock

| Type | Key Feature | 1st Step |
|---|---|---|
| Hypovolaemic | Most common cause: haemorrhage | **Fluid** |
| Neurogenic | Only type causing **bradycardia** | Fluid |
| Septic | Only type with **increased CO** | Fluid → then antibiotics |
| Cardiogenic | Only type with **increased CVP** | **Inotropes** |

- **Fluid of choice for resuscitation:** Normal saline or lactated Ringer's
- **Urgent blood:** Packed RBCs (O− low haemolysin)
- Normal CVP: **8–12 cm**
- ⚡ Post-op CVP = 2 → **fluid**; Post-MI CVP = 16 or low BP → **inotropes**

## Burns

**Rule of Nines:**
| Area | % |
|---|---|
| Head and neck | 9% |
| Whole arm | 9% (front = 4.5%) |
| Front chest + abdomen | 18% |
| Back chest + abdomen | 18% |
| Whole leg | 18% (front only = 9%) |
| Perineum | 1% |

**Depth:**
| Degree | Layers | Pain |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | Epidermis only | Most painful |
| 2nd | Epidermis + part of dermis | Painful |
| 3rd (full thickness) | Whole skin | **Painless + white** |

**Complications:**
1. **Inhalation → laryngeal oedema** → immediate intubation
   - ⚡ Soot in airway → intubate; Husky/soft voice → intubate; Burn to head/neck → intubate
2. **Stress ulcer**
3. **Infection** — timing: 5–7 days — organism: **pseudomonas**
4. **Eschars** → immediate **escharotomy** (NOT fasciotomy)

**Management — ABC first:**
- Check airway first (intubate if needed)
- **Fluid: Parkland formula** → Lactated Ringer's: **4 ml × burn% × body weight (kg)**
  - 50% in first 8 hours; 50% in next 16 hours
- Best fluid in burns → **lactated Ringer's**

**Prognosis (most important → least):**
1. Surface area ← most important
2. Depth
3. Site (face = very serious)

## Central Venous Catheter Complications

| Complication | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Infection** (most common) | Organism: staph — pain + pus → **remove catheter** |
| Pneumothorax | — |
| Thrombosis (upper limb) | Inv: duplex |

**During line removal:**
- Dislodgment of thrombus
- Vessel injury → marked swelling of face and neck → **Inv: CT with contrast**

## Miscellaneous

---

**Necrotising Fasciitis:**
- **Risk:** DM, severe infection
- **Organism:** *Streptococcus pyogenes*
- ⚡ 1st step: **Debridement** + antibiotics
- Cellulitis on ABX + getting worse + black spots on image → **debridement**

**Gas Gangrene:**
- **Organism:** *Clostridium perfringens*
- **C/P:** Lacerated wound + gas + swelling + pain + numbness + black muscle + offensive odour
- ⚡ 1st step: **Debridement** + antibiotics

---

**Hazards of Splenectomy:**
- At risk of: pneumococcal, meningococcal, *Haemophilus influenzae* infections

**Prophylaxis:**
| Vaccine/Drug | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Pneumococcal** ← most important | 2–3 weeks before surgery + every 5 years |
| Long-acting penicillin | — |
| Influenza vaccine | — |
| Meningococcal vaccine | — |

---

**Wound Dehiscence:**
- **Timing:** Usually **1 week** after surgery
- ⚡ **Key word:** Serosanguinous discharge
- **Risk factors:** Paralytic ileus, haematoma
- **Complication:** Evisceration
- No evisceration → conservative (abdominal strapping)
- **Evisceration → urgent surgery**

---

**Tetanus Prophylaxis in Wounds:**

⚡ When to give TIG — **BOTH conditions must be met:**
1. Lacerated (tetanus-prone) wound **AND**
2. Not vaccinated / unknown / <3 doses

**Lacerated wound (most common exam scenario):**
| Vaccination Status | Tetanus Vaccine | TIG |
|---|---|---|
| Vaccinated (≥3 doses) | Only if last dose **>5 years** | **No** |
| Not vaccinated / unknown / <3 doses | **Yes** | **Yes** |

**Clean wound:**
| Vaccination Status | Tetanus Vaccine | TIG |
|---|---|---|
| Vaccinated (≥3 doses) | Only if last dose **>10 years** | **No** |
| Not vaccinated / unknown / <3 doses | **Yes** | **No** |

**Tetanus-prone wounds:** Compound fractures, bites, deep penetrating, burns, soil-contaminated, extensive tissue damage

**1st step in any lacerated wound → Debridement**

---

**Cancer Tongue:**
- **Patient:** Old age male smoker
- **Type:** SCC — premalignant lesion = **leukoplakia**
- **C/P:** Blood-stained saliva, dysphagia, enlarged cervical LN, **pain referred to ear**
- **Inv:** Biopsy (choice) → if positive → **CT head and neck** for metastasis
- ⚡ Old male smoker + any tongue complaint or ear pain → **cancer tongue until proved otherwise**
- ⚡ Any head and neck cancer → must do **CT head and neck** to assess metastasis
- ⚡ Lump on tonsil + alcoholic + smoker → **SCC**

---

**Cancer Larynx:**
- Middle-aged male smoker + hoarseness of voice
- **Most common risk factor:** Smoking
- **Inv:** Indirect laryngoscopy + biopsy

---

**Insulinoma — Whipple's Triad:**
1. Attacks of hypoglycaemia
2. Blood glucose low during attack
3. Symptoms relieved by glucose

---

**Rectus Sheath Haematoma:**
- **C/P:** Abdominal pain referred to back + hypovolaemic shock + pallor
- **Inv:** CT
- **TTT:** Conservative (resuscitation = 1st step)
- If on warfarin → **immediate FFP**

---

**Zenker's (Pharyngeal) Diverticulum:**
- **Patient:** Old age male
- **C/P:** Dysphagia + regurgitation + swelling and **gurgling in the neck**
- **Inv:** Barium swallow
- **TTT:** Myotomy + excision OR endoscopic stapling

---

## ⚡ Debridement — Universal 1st Step (Recap)
All of the following → **debridement first:**
- Diabetic foot
- Lacerated wound
- Open fracture
- Gas gangrene / necrotising fasciitis
- Cellulitis worsening on antibiotics
- Scrotal gangrene (Fournier's)
